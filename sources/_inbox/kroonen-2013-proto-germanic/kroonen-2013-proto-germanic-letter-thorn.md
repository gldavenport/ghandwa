An old stative formation identical to Lat. tacere. In Go. pahan the effects of Verner's law have been removed, but it is unclear on the basis of what form that could have happened; duratives always had zero grade of the root and a stressed -ehi- suffix. *paggon- w.v. 'to silence' - ON pagga w.v. 'id.', Nw. tagga w.v. 'id.' => *tHk-neh₂- (WEUR).

An interesting formation. Formally, the verb looks like the iterative (3sg. *pakkopi, 3pl. *pagunanpi < *tHk-neh₂-ti, *tHk-nhi-entl) to *pagen-, but se mantically it functions as a causative-factitive ('to put to silence'). *Pahsu- m. 'badger' - MDu. das m. 'id.', Du. das c. 'id.', MHG dahs m. 'id.', G Dachs m. 'id.' => *ta ks- (WEUR/NIE).

Most WGm. forms can straightforwardly be derived from *dahsa-, but dial. Nw. toks implies ON *p9x < *pahsu-. This u-stem may have split off from a dat.pl. of an n-stem *pahsan- (cf. *uhsan- 'ox') that can be postulated on the basis of the late Latin loanword taxo (beside taxus ), the source of Fr. dial. taisson, It. tasso, Sp. tej6n 'badger' (EWAhd: II, 498-501) and possibly also Basque dial. azkoin 'id.' Unrelated to Hitt. tasku-, an unspecified subcaudal bodypart (pace Katz 1998).
