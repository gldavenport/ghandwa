Rule-by-rule tracking of source-literature phonological rules against Ghandwa's implementation. For each rule: what the source says, what PGmc/PItal/PC did, and what Ghandwa does or doesn't do.

## Part I — Ringe 2017 PGmc Comparanda

For each shared rule, the PGmc reflex and Ringe's example(s). Rules that diverge from Ringe are noted.

---

### Stage 0

#### Centumization: ḱ→k, ǵ→g, ǵʰ→gʰ
PGmc shares this as a co-development of the centum node. Not a separate rule in Ringe — the palatals had already merged with plain velars before the PGmc period.

#### w-absorption: Kw→kʷ
PGmc: *Kw sequences merged with inherited labiovelars (Ringe §2.2.3(ii)). E.g.:
- *ék´wos 'horse' (kw from k+w sequence) > PGmc *ehwaz

#### Boukólos rule: Kʷ→K adjacent to u/ū/w
PGmc: labiovelars delabialized next to *u (Ringe §2.2.3(ii)):
- *gwráh₂u- 'heavy' → *gwr̥h₂ús > *gwurús > PGmc *kuruz (Goth. kaúrus)
- *gwhénti- → *gwhn̥tís > *gwhúntis > PGmc *gunþiz 'battle'
- *dn̥g´hwáh₂- 'tongue' → *dunghwā- > *dungwhā- > *dunghā- >! PGmc *tungōn-

#### KʷC→K: labiovelar delabializes before consonant
PGmc: labiovelars delabialized before *t (Ringe §2.2.3(ii)):
- *nókʷt- ~ *nékʷt- 'night' > *nókt- > PGmc *naht- (Goth. nahts, OHG naht)
Note: Ringe finds no clear Germanic examples before *s.

---

### Stage 1

#### T{T,s}→ss: dental before dental or s → geminate ss
PGmc: PIE surface cluster *tst (underlying */T+t/) → PGmc *ss (Ringe §2.2.4(i)):
- *widstós 'known' > PGmc *(ga)wissaz 'certain' (OE ġewiss)
- *sedstós 'seated' > PGmc *sessaz 'seat' (ON, OE sess)
- *wédhstis 'act of joining' > PGmc *ga-wissiz 'joint' (Goth. gawiss)
- pre-PGmc *gwétstis 'act of speaking' > PGmc *ga-kwissiz 'agreement' (Goth. gaqiss)

Simplified to *s before a long vowel, diphthong, or consonant:
- pre-Gmc *káydstis 'act of calling' > PGmc *haistiz (with simplification)

#### ssC→sC: geminate ss simplifies before consonant
Follows from the above — see Ringe §2.2.4(i). Feeds the T{T,s} rule outputs.

#### Thorn metathesis: TK→KT word-internally
**DIVERGES FROM RINGE 2017.** Ringe 2017 gives TK→KS as the PGmc-path outcome. Our rule follows Jasanoff 2018 (via Ringe 2024) giving TK→KT (Greek-type depalatalization). PGmc lost the dental entirely, leaving only K, then Grimm's Law applied.
- PIE *h₂ŕ̥tḱos > *arktos (Ghandwa, Greek-type) vs. PGmc *b-e-r-ō 'bear' (euphemism, original form not preserved)

#### Laryngeal coloring: h₂→a, h₃→o
PGmc parallel: same coloring occurred, then laryngeals were lost (Ringe §2.2.1):
- *h₂éḱweh₂ → PIE *akwā > PGmc *ahwō 'water' (cf. Goth. aƕa, OE ēa)
- *ph₂tēr 'father' → *pətēr > PGmc *fadēr (ON faðir, OE fæder)

#### VHV→V̄: identical vowels across laryngeal contract
PGmc: contraction of vowels in hiatus generally; overlong/trimoric vowels result when laryngeal had separated them (Ringe §2.2.1(ii)):
- *séh₁mn̥ 'seed' > PGmc *sēmō (Goth. saian, OHG sāmo)

#### VH→V̄: nonhigh vowel before laryngeal lengthens
PGmc: same (Ringe §2.2.1(ii)):
- *séh₁mn̥ 'seed' → *sēmn̥ > PGmc *sēmō (Goth. saian)
- *bhráh₂tēr 'brother' > PGmc *brōþēr (Goth. broþar, OE brōþor)
- *dhéh₁ti- 'act of putting' > PGmc *dēdiz

#### H→∅ word-initially
PGmc: same (Ringe §2.2.1(i)):
- *h₁esti '(s)he is' > PGmc *isti (Goth. ist, OE is)
- *h₁ed- 'to eat' > PGmc *etaną (Goth. itan, OE etan)
- *h₂ánti 'in front of' > PGmc *andi (OE and, OHG enti)

#### H between consonants → a (initial syllable) / ∅ (post-vocalic syllable)
PGmc: epenthetic *ə next to noninitial laryngeals between nonsyllabics; laryngeal lost, *ə eventually → *a (Ringe §2.2.1(iv)):
- *ph₂tēr 'father' > *pətēr > PGmc *fadēr (ON faðir, OE fæder)
- *kh₂piéti '(s)he is grasping' > *kəpiéti > PGmc *habiþi (ON hefja, OE hebban)

#### Voiced obstruent → voiceless before t or s
PGmc: same process feeds Grimm's Law (Ringe §2.2.4(i)); *sd examples:
- *h₃ósdos 'branch' > *ósdos > PGmc *astaz (Goth. asts, OHG ast)
- *nisdós 'nest' > PGmc *nistaz (OE nest)

#### Osthoff's Law: V̄RC→VRC
PGmc: same (Ringe §2.2.3(iii)):
- *h₁nḗh₃mn̥ 'name' → *nēmn̥ → *nemn̥ (by Osthoff) > PGmc *namô (Goth. namo, OE nama)
- *pēŕs-n- 'heel' > PGmc *fersn- (Goth. faírzna, OHG fersana)
- *h₂yuHṅkós 'young' → *yūnkós → *yunkós (by Osthoff) > PGmc *jungaz (Goth. juggs, OE ġeong)

### Stage 2

#### Syllabic resonants: r̥→ar, l̥→al, m̥→am, n̥→an
PGmc develops *uR instead of *aR (Ringe §2.2.2(i)):
- *sm̥H- 'summer' > PGmc *sumaraz (OE sumor) [Ghandwa: *samar-]
- *dék´m̥d 'ten' > PGmc *tehun (Goth. taíhun) [Ghandwa: *tekam]
- *k´m̥tóm 'hundred' > PGmc *hundą (Goth. hunda) [Ghandwa: *kantom]
- *wlk̥´wos 'wolf' > PGmc *wulfaz [Ghandwa: *walkʷos]
- *dr̥k´tós 'visible' > PGmc *turhtaz (OE torht) [Ghandwa: *darktos]
- *k´r̥n- 'horn' > PGmc *hurną (Goth. haúrn, OE horn) [Ghandwa: *karnom]
- *n̥- 'un-' > PGmc *un- [Ghandwa: *an-]

**Key divergence**: PGmc has *uR (vowel before resonant); Ghandwa has *aR (vowel after resonant). Both are well-attested IE outcomes — Greek, Armenian, and Indo-Iranian generally show the vowel before; Celtic and to some extent Italic show variable outcomes.

#### Aspirates → voiced fricatives: bʰ→β, dʰ→ð, gʰ→ɣ, gʷʰ→ɣʷ
PGmc: same as third part of Grimm's Law (Ringe §2.2.4(i)):
- *bhráh₂tēr 'brother' > PGmc *brōþēr (Goth. broþar)
- *dék´m̥d 'ten' > PGmc *tehun (dʰ → ð/d)
- *gwhen- 'strike, kill' > PGmc *banō 'murderer' (gʷʰ → b/β)
- *ánteros 'other' > PGmc *anþaraz (Goth. anþar, OE ōþer)

Note: Ghandwa stops at voiced fricatives [β, ð, ɣ, ɣʷ]; PGmc went further, eventually devoicing some and merging others. But the initial step — breathy-voiced stops → voiced fricatives — is the same change.

#### s→z between voiced sounds
PGmc: Verner's Law is the PGmc parallel, though the conditioning environment differs (Verner conditions on PIE accent position, not purely on voicing of adjacent segments). The surface result is similar — *s became *z between voiced sounds in many environments — but the mechanism is accent-conditioned in PGmc rather than purely phonotactic.
- *snusós 'daughter-in-law' > PGmc *snuzō (OE snoru, OHG snura) — s→z between voiced vowels
- *woséyeti '(s)he clothes' > *wozijiþi >! PGmc *waziþi (OE wereþ, OHG werit)

#### y→j: palatal glide respelling
This is a Ghandwa orthographic convention, not a sound change rule per se. In PGmc *j- is the inherited representation of PIE *y-.

---

### Ringe Notes

- **Syllabic resonant vocalism** is the clearest typological divergence: Ghandwa *aR vs. PGmc *uR. Both are legitimate IE outcomes.
- **Thorn rule** diverges entirely: Ghandwa follows Jasanoff 2018 (TK→KT, Greek-type); PGmc lost the dental, leaving K, then Grimm's Law applied.
- **s→z rule**: Ghandwa's rule is purely phonotactic (voiced environment); PGmc's Verner's Law is accent-conditioned. Surface results often coincide but the rules are formally different.
- **Grimm's Law and Verner's Law** are PGmc-specific and have no Ghandwa equivalents. Ghandwa has aspirates→voiced fricatives (one-step), which corresponds only to the third part of Grimm's Law.

## Part II — PItal/Celtic Comparanda (De Vaan 2009 & Swanenvleugel 2021)

Rules attested for Proto-Italo-Celtic (PIC), Proto-Italic (PItal), or Proto-Celtic (PC) that are **not yet in Ghandwa** or differ from Ghandwa's current implementation. These are candidates for daughter-language innovations or staged refinements.

### Already in Ghandwa — for reference

The following PIC/PItal/PC rules are already implemented and not discussed further here:

| Rule | Source | Ghandwa status |
|---|---|---|
| Centumization ḱ→k, ǵ→g | DV 0.0 / Sw -3 | ✓ Pre-stage |
| Laryngeal coloring HV→V | DV 1.1 / Sw -2 | ✓ Stage 1 |
| VH→V̄ (including iH→ī, uH→ū) | DV 1.2–1.3 / Sw -1 | ✓ Stage 1 |
| CHC→CaC | DV 2.3 / Sw 7 | ✓ Stage 1 |
| Osthoff's Law V̄RC→VRC | DV 2.1 | ✓ Stage 1 |
| Aspirates→voiced fricatives | Sw 0 (partial) | ✓ Stage 2 (Ghandwa extends to all four: bʰ→β, dʰ→ð, gʰ→ɣ, gʷʰ→ɣʷ) |

**Note on CRHC:** Both sources give CRHC→CRāC (with compensatory lengthening). Ghandwa gives CRaRC (vowel after resonant, no length). This is a known divergence — Ghandwa aligns with Celtic metathesis order but without the compensatory length, on the grounds that H drops before resonant vocalization rather than feeding it. Not listed below as a gap because the Ghandwa decision is deliberate.

### Rules Not in Ghandwa — Candidate Pool

#### DV 1.4 — Dental affrication before dental: Tt→Tˢt

**Source:** De Vaan 2009, stage 1.4. Also noted with uncertainty for *-ddʰ-→-dˢd-*.

**Description:** A dental stop before another dental becomes an affricate (sibilant-dental). This is the pre-stage of what feeds Latin *-ss-* and Celtic *-ss-* (via Tˢt→ss). Ghandwa goes directly to T{T,s}→ss, skipping the affricate intermediate.

**Relevance:** If a daughter language is modeled as going through a transparent affricate stage before simplification to *ss*, DV's Tˢ intermediate could be built in. Also relevant for daughters that retain affricates rather than simplifying.

**Daughter potential:** High. A daughter with /ts/ or /dz/ as phonemes could derive them from this stage. A daughter that produces *-ss-* would need this plus a subsequent Tˢt→ss rule.

---

#### DV 2.2 — Pius-rule: uHijV→wiHjV

**Source:** De Vaan 2009, stage 2.2.

**Description:** Reordering of a *u* before a laryngeal-glide-vowel sequence — the *u* becomes a *w* glide and the laryngeal shifts rightward. Primary motivation is Latin *pius* (< *piHios*?) and a small class of similar forms.

**Relevance:** Very narrow environment. Ghandwa's current pipeline would either produce a ˀ warning or fall through. Not a pressing gap for most lexical items.

**Daughter potential:** Low priority unless a daughter inherits forms where this environment surfaces. Worth flagging for the *pius*-type lexical set when those words are added.

---

#### DV 2.5/2.6 — Environment-split for laryngeal-colored resonants

**Source:** De Vaan 2009, stages 2.5–2.6.

**Description:** De Vaan distinguishes the general CRHC→CRāC from:
- **2.5:** CRHTC→CRaTC (Italic) / CRHt/s→CRat/s (Celtic) — laryngeal before dental or *s* gives short *a*, not long *ā*
- **2.6:** RHT/s→RaT/s — same short-vowel outcome when there is no initial consonant

**The split:** The general rule gives *ā* (long); before a following dental or *s*, the vowel is short *a* instead. This is conditioned by the following consonant.

**Relevance:** Ghandwa currently has no environment-sensitivity in CRHC. All cases give *CRaRC* (with Ghandwa's metathesis). The Italic/Celtic split would predict that daughters might surface *CRāRC* in the general case and *CRaRC* before dentals/s — or vice versa depending on which metathesis order they inherit.

**Daughter potential:** Medium-high. This is a natural daughter-differentiation point: one daughter could generalize the long vowel, another the short, and a third split by environment.

---

#### DV 2.7 / Sw 4 — Syllabic resonant adjacent to voiced stop

**Source:** De Vaan 2009, stage 2.7 (*R̥DC→RaDC*); Swanenvleugel 2021, stage 4 (*RD̉C→RHDC*).

**Description:** These two sources handle the same environment — a syllabic resonant adjacent to a voiced stop — but in opposite directions:
- **De Vaan:** R̥DC → RaDC — resonant vocalizes with *a* inserted before the voiced stop
- **Swanenvleugel:** treats this as a pre-resolution laryngeal insertion (RHDC — the H is posited before vocalizing)

**Ghandwa's current state:** R̥ vocalizes uniformly to *aR* regardless of following consonant. The voiced-stop environment is not distinguished.

**Relevance:** This cluster type does surface in Ghandwa (e.g. forms with *-r̥d-*, *-n̥g-* etc.). The daughters could diverge in how they handle it.

**Daughter potential:** High for daughters with distinct voiced-stop cluster behavior. The two sources' disagreement also leaves interpretive room — a daughter could implement either the De Vaan surface result or the Swanenvleugel intermediate.

---

#### DV 2.8 — Four-consonant cluster breaking: CCCC→CaCCC

**Source:** De Vaan 2009, stage 2.8.

**Description:** A sequence of four consonants is broken by *a*-epenthesis after the first consonant.

**Ghandwa's current state:** No such rule. Four-consonant clusters trigger a CCC warning (three or more), but no resolution is specified.

**Relevance:** Low frequency but could surface in complex nominal or verbal stems. The CCC cluster warning in Ghandwa's transformer is the diagnostic entry point for these.

**Daughter potential:** Medium. A phonologically conservative daughter might retain the clusters; a more innovative one would apply epenthesis. The epenthetic vowel quality (*a*) aligns with Ghandwa's general epenthetic default.

---

#### Sw 5 — Deglottalization of voiced mediae

**Source:** Swanenvleugel 2021, stage 5.

**Description:** Swanenvleugel posits that the PIE *mediae* (*b, d, g* etc.) were underlyingly glottalized (a glottalic theory assumption) and that deglottalization was a PIC stage.

**Relevance:** This reflects a different underlying theory of PIE phonology than Ghandwa uses. Ghandwa operates on the standard laryngeal theory with plain voiced stops. **Not applicable** to Ghandwa's framework.

**Daughter potential:** None — framework-internal to Swanenvleugel's model.

---

#### Sw 9 — Labial-labiovelar dissimilation: p…kʷ→kʷ…kʷ

**Source:** Swanenvleugel 2021, stage 9 (unordered).

**Description:** When *p* and *kʷ* are both present in a word, the *p* assimilates to *kʷ*. The classic example is *penkʷe* 'five' → *kʷenkʷe* (Latin *quinque*, Welsh *pump* < *kʷinkʷe*).

**Ghandwa's current state:** Not implemented. Ghandwa preserves *p* and *kʷ* freely.

**Decision (2026-03-14):** Ghandwa does **not** apply this rule. It is a daughter-level possible change. Daughters that align with Italic/Celtic for 'five' must apply it independently.

**Daughter potential:** High. Daughters inheriting Ghandwa *penkʷe* can apply this to derive *kʷenkʷe* forms.

---

#### Sw 10 — Cluster simplification: mw→w

**Source:** Swanenvleugel 2021, stage 10 (unordered).

**Description:** The cluster *mw* simplifies to *w*, losing the nasal.

**Ghandwa's current state:** Not implemented. *mw* clusters are not addressed.

**Relevance:** Could surface in Ghandwa forms with *m*-final stems followed by *w*-initial suffixes or enclitics. Frequency is low but non-zero.

**Daughter potential:** Low-medium. A natural phonotactic simplification that a daughter could apply. Alternatively, a daughter might instead nasalize the *w* or insert a vowel.

---

#### Sw 11 — Nasal assimilation: mj→nj

**Source:** Swanenvleugel 2021, stage 11 (unordered).

**Description:** The cluster *mj* (bilabial nasal + palatal glide) assimilates to *nj* (coronal nasal + palatal glide), anticipating the palatal place of the glide.

**Ghandwa's current state:** Not implemented. Ghandwa explicitly has no nasal assimilation as a general policy.

**Relevance:** The specific *mj→nj* environment is narrower than general nasal assimilation. Could surface at morpheme boundaries (e.g. *m*-stem + *-yo-* suffix).

**Daughter potential:** Medium. A natural palatalization-driven assimilation; plausible for a daughter with active palatal harmony or *yod*-mutation processes.

---

#### Sw 12 — Yod sequences: ei̯e→ii̯e / i̯e→i

**Source:** Swanenvleugel 2021, stage 12 (unordered).

**Description:** Two related rules: (a) *ei̯e* raises to *ii̯e*; (b) *i̯e* simplifies to *i*. Together they collapse certain *e*-colored sequences before *y*+vowel.

**Ghandwa's current state:** Not implemented. VV hiatus from laryngeal loss is a noted unresolved tension; *iye* sequences are not specifically targeted.

**Relevance:** Primarily affects verbal and adjectival *-yo-* stems. Would interact with Ghandwa's *y→j* respelling convention.

**Daughter potential:** Medium-high for daughters with active *yod* coalescence. This could feed palatal consonant development in a daughter.

---

#### Sw 13 — Sibilant dissimilation before *r*: sr→θr

**Source:** Swanenvleugel 2021, stage 13 (unordered).

**Description:** *s* before *r* dissimilates to a dental fricative *θ* (theta). This is the source of Latin *fr-* in some words (via *θr→fr*) and of Celtic *θr* sequences.

**Ghandwa's current state:** Not implemented. *sr* clusters are not addressed and would surface as-is.

**Relevance:** Interesting for Ghandwa daughters that already have dental fricatives (Ghandwa itself has *ð* from aspirates). The *sr→θr* rule could be particularly natural for a daughter that extends fricative distribution. Also relevant because *sr*-initial roots (e.g. flow-words, star-words) are lexically common.

**Daughter potential:** High for a daughter with an active dental fricative. Could also diverge: one daughter *sr→θr→fr* (Italic-like), another *sr→θr* (stops), another preserves *sr*.

### Decisions Flagged as Urgent

1. ~~**p…kʷ→kʷ…kʷ (Sw 9):**~~ **Resolved (2026-03-14).** Ghandwa does not apply this. Daughter-level only.

2. **R̥DC environment (DV 2.7 / Sw 4):** Ghandwa's uniform R̥→aR doesn't distinguish voiced-stop following environments. Whether this matters depends on how many lexical items surface with *-R̥D-* clusters. Worth auditing the lexicon.

3. **sr→θr (Sw 13):** If any daughter has dental fricatives as a productive class (likely, given Ghandwa's *ð*), *sr* clusters are an immediate pressure point. Audit *sr*-initial forms in the lexicon.
