# Ghandwa Historical-Geographic Model — Integration Handoff

## Core Conceptual Shift

The Ghandwa project has evolved away from a rigid tree model toward a:

- dialect-continuum model
- interaction-sphere model
- Bronze Age prestige-network model
- later Iron Age crystallization model

The key realization is that “Proto-” languages need not represent perfectly uniform speech communities. Common Ghandwa should instead be viewed as a reconstructible shared layer within a broader dialect pool.

---

# 1. Historical Periodization

## Late/Common Ghandwa

Late/Common Ghandwa is best modeled as:

- a Bronze Age maritime-river civilization
- with coastal and delta trade networks
- connected by major river systems
- maintaining a prestige koine / elite dialect continuum

This phase resembles:

- Proto-Celtic before Q/P crystallization
- Proto-Greek before historical dialect differentiation
- broad IE interaction spheres such as Corded Ware / Bell Beaker continua

The Bronze Age network itself helps maintain linguistic coherence.

---

## Iron Age Crystallization

The daughter branches crystallize later, likely during an Iron Age transition involving:

- regional militarization
- inland/upriver expansion
- reduced maritime integration
- stronger regional elites
- weakening of old trade/river links

This causes:

- dialect hardening
- regional phonological regularization
- divergent innovation spread
- formation of Western / Central / Eastern Ghandwic

Iron technology itself may partially drive this fragmentation.

---

# 2. Geographic Model

## Core Settlement Pattern

The current preferred model:

- Ghandwic speakers first establish themselves in a major delta/coastal zone
- expansion proceeds upriver along multiple river systems
- separate river basins create distinct dialect trajectories over time

This creates:

- prolonged dialect chains
- overlapping innovation zones
- substrate contact variation
- mixed central dialects
- isolated edge dialects

---

## Shoreline + River Economy

The preferred economic model combines:

- maritime trade
- riverine penetration
- coastal prestige networks
- inland tributary settlement

This allows:

- distant coastal settlements to remain linguistically connected
- inland valleys to diverge more rapidly
- central confluence cities to preserve mixed dialects

---

## Septakvās

Septakvās (“Seven Rivers”) now fits naturally as:

- a massive confluence metropolis
- prestige center of Late Common Ghandwa
- cosmopolitan Bronze Age river-maritime city
- source of koineization and mixed dialect layering

Central Ghandwic may preserve the most direct continuation of this metropolitan dialect layer.

---

# 3. Renaming the Daughter Branches

The A/B/C system is no longer conceptually adequate.

Preferred replacement:

- Western Ghandwic
- Central Ghandwic
- Eastern Ghandwic

This better reflects:

- geography
- dialect continua
- innovation flow
- edge-vs-center dynamics
- historical migration logic

---

# 4. Daughter Branch Dynamics

## Western Ghandwic

General profile:

- more conservative
- inland/river-valley orientation
- stronger delabialization tendencies
- simplification of fricative systems through devoicing

Key tendencies:

```text
Kʷ > K
β ð ɣ ɣʷ > ɸ θ x xʷ
```

---

## Central Ghandwic

General profile:

- transitional/mixed dialect
- crossroads/confluence geography
- preserves awkward intermediate systems
- incomplete innovation spread

This branch is increasingly viewed as “Greek-like” structurally:

- between stronger edge systems
- mixed archaism + innovation
- partial retention of older contrasts
- contradictory-looking isoglosses

### Labiovelars

Central Ghandwic preserves labiovelars overall but weakens them selectively.

Examples:

```text
ɣʷ > w /V_V, #_V, _V# environments
ɣʷ > gʷ /N_
Kʷ retained overall
Kʷ > K in selected environments
```

This contrasts with:

- Western delabialization (Kʷ > K)
- Eastern develarization (Kʷ > P)

---

### Voiced Fricatives

Current preferred model:

```text
β ð ɣ ɣʷ > b d g gʷ / N_

β ð ɣ ɣʷ > ɸ θ x xʷ / #_V
β ð ɣ ɣʷ > ɸ θ x xʷ / _{s,t}

β ð ɣ ɣʷ retained elsewhere
```

This produces an intentionally awkward transitional system.

Later Central-Ghandwic daughters may regularize this differently:

- universal hardening to stops
- further lenition to h/∅
- partial retention
- mixed regional outcomes

---

## Eastern Ghandwic

General profile:

- frontier-expansion dialect
- stronger restructuring tendencies
- more aggressive labiovelar resolution
- possible stronger palatalization tendencies

Preferred labiovelar outcome direction:

```text
Kʷ > P / labial-type outcomes
```

Eastern Ghandwic functions as the “extreme edge” system compared to Central.

---

# 5. Dialect Geography Analogies

Several analogies are now structurally useful.

## Romance Analogy

The broad CWC-like continuum can be compared loosely to:

- the broader Romance dialect world

while Italo-Celtic-like subcontinua resemble:

- regional Romance clusters

Important caveat:

- the prehistoric IE system lacks Roman-style state infrastructure
- cohesion comes from mobility/trade/prestige networks instead

---

## Greek Analogy

Central Ghandwic increasingly resembles Greek structurally:

- intermediate geographic position
- mixed innovation pressures
- partial preservation of older systems
- incomplete regularization
- prolonged labiovelar retention

---

# 6. Italo-Celtic / Dialect-Pool Insights

## General Model

Current working interpretation:

- Italo-Celtic may represent a west-central/southern dialect belt
- Celtic may represent a northern/western continuation
- Italic may represent a southern/Adriatic-facing continuation

This is not a clean branch split but a gradual dialect crystallization.

---

## Q/P Celtic

Q-Celtic vs P-Celtic should be treated as:

- post-Common-Celtic crystallizations
- not necessarily primordial branch divisions

---

## Labiovelar Developments

Important realization:

- Kʷ > P is typologically natural and independently repeatable
- shared Kʷ > P does not itself prove close subgrouping

However:

- areal/dialect-chain influence remains plausible

---

# 7. PIE Reconstruction Methodology Insights

## Reconstruction as Dialect Recovery

Constructing a PIE daughter has revealed that reconstructed proto-languages may preserve:

- dialect residue
- competing derivations
- register layering
- fossilized morphology
- contact effects

Proto-languages should not always be treated as perfectly uniform.

---

## Heteroclitics

The old r/n heteroclitic system was likely:

- productive very early
- later fragmented unevenly among daughters

Many strange daughter outcomes may reflect:

- separate derivations
- stem-family expansion
- analogical remodeling
- dialect-specific lexicalization

rather than one perfectly preserved inherited paradigm.

---

## Strange Ablaut

Potential methodological caution:

Some unusual PIE ablaut patterns may reflect:

- separate derivations from related roots
- dialectal variants
- lexical doublets

rather than one inherited paradigm.

# 10. Geographic Innovation Grid

One emerging conceptual model is to treat the Ghandwic world as a partially connected innovation grid rather than a simple linear chain.

Possible heuristic structure:

```text
Northwest   North-Central   Northeast
West        Central         East
Southwest   South-Central   Southeast
```

Different innovations may originate in different geographic sectors and spread unevenly across the network.

Examples:

- northern prestige/maritime innovations
- eastern frontier restructuring
- southern Adriatic/coastal contact effects
- western conservative inland retention
- central koineization and mixed systems

This allows:

- overlapping isoglosses
- contradictory-looking subgroup signals
- delayed or partial innovation spread
- multiple prestige centers
- secondary convergence zones

The model also better captures how real dialect continua behave:

- some changes spread primarily along coasts
- some follow river systems
- some stop at mountains or forests
- some move through elite trade networks rather than simple adjacency

This may eventually replace overly linear “West/Central/East” thinking with a more realistic network geography while still preserving the usefulness of the primary Western/Central/Eastern Ghandwic groupings.
