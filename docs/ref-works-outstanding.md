# Substrate / Wanderword Reference Sources

## Proto-Dravidian / Dravidian comparative phonology
- Krishnamurti, Bhadriraju. *The Dravidian Languages*. Cambridge Language Surveys, 2003. (in progress)
- Subrahmanyam, P.S. *Dravidian Comparative Phonology*. Annamalai University, 1983. — free on Internet Archive
- Zvelebil, Kamil. *Dravidian Linguistics: An Introduction*. Pondicherry Institute of Linguistics and Culture, 1990. — free on Internet Archive
- Burrow, T. & Emeneau, M.B. *A Dravidian Etymological Dictionary* (DEDR). 2nd rev. ed., 1984. — not a purchase target; browsable at dsal.uchicago.edu/dictionaries/burrow/ (blocks automated queries, human use only)
- Southworth, Franklin. "Proto-Dravidian Agriculture." ESCA Round Table conference paper, 2005. — free PDF, self-archived: sas.upenn.edu/~fsouth/from_ccat/Proto-DravidianAgriculture.pdf

## Contact linguistics
- Hock, Hans Henrich & Joseph, Brian D. *Language History, Language Change, and Language Relationship*. 2nd rev. ed., De Gruyter Mouton, 2009. (in progress)

## Pre-Greek / Aegean substrate
- Beekes, Robert S.P. *Pre-Greek: Phonology, Morphology, Lexicon*. Brill, 2014.
- Beekes, Robert S.P. *Etymological Dictionary of Greek*. 2 vols., Brill, 2010. — expensive; check library access first
- Furnée, Edzard J. *Die wichtigsten konsonantischen Erscheinungen des Vorgriechischen*. Den Haag, 1972. — out of print, German; ILL target, not a buy
- Steele, Philippa M. *A Linguistic History of Ancient Cyprus*. Cambridge Classical Studies, 2013.
- Duhoux, Yves. *L'Étéocrétois: les textes – la langue*. Amsterdam: J.C. Gieben, 1982. — French
- Salgarella, Ester. *Aegean Linear Script(s): Rethinking the Relationship Between Linear A and Linear B*. Cambridge UP, 2020. — script context, not a decipherment
- Finkelberg, Margalit. *Greeks and Pre-Greeks*. Cambridge UP, 2005 (pbk. 2009). — historical/archaeological framing, lower priority

## Etruscan / Tyrsenian
- Wallace, Rex E. *Zikh Rasna: A Manual of the Etruscan Language and Inscriptions*. Beech Stave Press, 2008. — free on Internet Archive; preferred over Bonfante & Bonfante if only acquiring one
- Bonfante, Giuliano & Bonfante, Larissa. *The Etruscan Language: An Introduction*. Rev. ed., Manchester UP, 2002. — free on Internet Archive; cross-check etymological claims before citing, some chapters run speculative
