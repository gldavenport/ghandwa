# Crotonian and Crotonian+ Rule Sets

**Source:** Garnier & Sagot (2017), "A shared substrate between Greek and Italic," *Indogermanische Forschungen* 122(1): 29–60. DOI: 10.1515/if-2017-0002.

**Purpose of this document:** Working reference for a hypothetical PIE-derived donor language ("the donor") from which Ghandwa received loanwords as a substrate or adstrate. The Crotonian ruleset (§1) documents Garnier & Sagot's reconstructed phonological history as published. Crotonian+ (§3) is an extended working ruleset designed for donor language development: it incorporates an unpublished laryngeal rule attested in Garnier's own etymologies, generalizes rule 3 to all voiced aspirate + resonant clusters, and adds a front-diphthong monophthongization mirroring the attested back-diphthong rule. These additions are linguistically defensible extensions of principles already present in the Crotonian system; they are not attested in Garnier & Sagot and should be treated as design decisions for the donor, not claims about the historical Crotonian language.

## 1. Crotonian: Garnier & Sagot's Rule Set

Twelve rules ordered relative to each other as per Table 1 of Garnier & Sagot (2017). Notation follows Garnier: P = any stop; Pː = fortis counterpart; B = lenis (voiced fricative) counterpart; R = non-syllabic resonant (*r, *l, *m, *n*); H = any laryngeal; Dʰ = any voiced aspirate (*bʰ, *dʰ, *gʰ, *gʷʰ*).

| # | Rule | Environment / Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ḱ > K | Centum treatment; merges palatovelar with plain velar |
| 2 | CHC > CăC | Laryngeal between two consonants vocalizes as *ă*; applies to all three laryngeals |
| 3 | bʰR > bR | *bʰ* before non-syllabic resonant loses aspiration before rule 4 fires; bleeds rule 4 for this environment |
| 4 | (#)Dʰ > (#)T | All remaining voiced aspirates → voiceless stops; word-initial explicitly noted; word-internal application masked by rule 7 (fn. 40) |
| 5 | CVRC > CRVC | Metathesis of non-syllabic liquid past preceding vowel |
| 6 | #(s)klV > #(s)kəlV | Schwa epenthesis in word-initial *kl*- and *skl*- clusters |
| 7 | P > Pː / V́(R)(s)\_ and P > B / \_(R)(s)V́ | Verner-like: posttonic stop → fortis; pretonic stop → lenis (voiced fricative); generates systematic fortis/lenis alternations |
| 8 | Barytonesis | Systematic stress retraction to initial syllable; bleeds the conditioning environment for further Verner alternations |
| 9 | VPNV > VNBV | Nasal anticipation with concurrent stop lenition; parallel to Latin *lex unda* but applies to all stops, not dentals only |
| 10 | Cu̯r̥C > CurC | Resyllabification of labiovelar + syllabic resonant + consonant; parallels in Anatolian, Tocharian, Albanian |
| 11 | #pr > #br and #tr > #Tr | Word-initial cluster treatments; labial + *r* → voiced (lenis); dental + *r* → fortis; asymmetric |
| 12 | ow > ū | Monophthongization of back closing diphthong |

### Key structural relationships

- Rule 3 **bleeds** rule 4 for *bʰ*R environments: aspiration stripped before rule 4 can devoice.
- Rule 3 and rules 4+11 **converge** on the same output for word-initial *#bʰr*-: both paths yield *#br*-. Garnier (fn. 54) acknowledges this and suggests the *b* in such clusters may be a conditioned allophone of Substr. *\*p* rather than evidence of a truly independent rule.
- Rule 7 **masks** the output of rule 4 word-internally (fn. 40): a posttonic *T* from rule 4 becomes fortis *Tː*; a pretonic *T* becomes lenis *B*. The devoicing is therefore only directly visible at word boundaries and in posttonic position.
- Rule 8 **bleeds** further rule 7 alternations by fixing accent initially.

### Diagnostic signature against Ghandwa

| PIE source | Ghandwa outcome | Crotonian outcome | Diagnostic |
|---|---|---|---|
| \*Dʰ (any position) | voiced fricative (β, ð, ɣ) | voiceless stop (T or B via Verner) | strong |
| \*ow diphthong | preserved (-ou-) | ū | strong |
| CVRC sequence | CVRC preserved | CRVC (metathesis) | moderate |
| Laryngeal CHC | zero (absorbed) | ă | visible in some loans |

## 2. Excluded Laryngeal Rule

Garnier & Sagot discuss but do not include in Table 1 a second laryngeal vocalization rule, attested across multiple etymologies in §5.9:

> **PIE \*CeRH.C > Substr. \*CeRă.C**

A laryngeal following a resonant in a post-full-grade-*e* consonant cluster vocalizes as *ă*. All three laryngeals produce *ă* in this environment. Examples from the paper:

- PIE \*mélh₃-tr̥ 'beam' → Substr. \*mélaTr̥ (→ Gr. μέλαθρον)
- PIE \*pélh₂-tr̥ 'flatness' → Substr. \*pélătr̥ (→ Gr. πλέθρον, πέλεθρον)
- PIE \*gʷérh₃-tr̥ 'abyss' → Substr. \*gʷérăTr̥ (→ Gr. βάραθρον)

This rule operates in a distinct environment from rule 2 (CHC > CăC): rule 2 requires the laryngeal to be flanked by two consonants with no intervening vowel; this rule requires a full-grade vowel + resonant preceding the laryngeal. They are complementary, not overlapping. Garnier's omission from Table 1 is unexplained; the rule does real work across several of his strongest etymologies.

## 3. Crotonian+: Extended Working Rule Set

Modifications from Crotonian:

1. **Rule 2 split into 2a and 2b**, incorporating the excluded laryngeal rule as a formal numbered step.
2. **Rule 3 generalized** from *bʰ*R > *b*R to DʰR > DR, covering all voiced aspirates before non-syllabic resonants. The bleeding relationship with rule 4 is preserved and extended: rule 3 now starves rule 4 of *all* DʰR inputs, not just *bʰ*R inputs. The convergence with rules 4+11 at word-initial position noted by Garnier applies equally to *#dʰr*- and *#gʰr*- clusters.
3. **Rule 13 added**: ej > ī, mirroring rule 12 (ow > ū). Front and back closing diphthongs both monophthongize to long high vowels. Typologically well-attested (cf. Latin *ei* > *ī*; Oscan-Umbrian parallel).

| # | Rule | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ḱ > K | Centum |
| 2a | CHC > CăC | Laryngeal between two consonants |
| 2b | CeRH.C > CeRă.C | Laryngeal after resonant in post-*e* cluster; not in Garnier's Table 1 |
| 3 | DʰR > DR | Generalized from Garnier's *bʰ*R > *b*R; bleeds rule 4 for all DʰR inputs |
| 4 | (#)Dʰ > (#)T | Remaining voiced aspirates (those not consumed by rule 3) → voiceless |
| 5 | CVRC > CRVC | Liquid metathesis |
| 6 | #(s)klV > #(s)kəlV | Schwa epenthesis |
| 7 | P > Pː / V́(R)(s)\_ and P > B / \_(R)(s)V́ | Verner-like fortis/lenis alternation |
| 8 | Barytonesis | Stress retraction |
| 9 | VPNV > VNBV | Nasal anticipation |
| 10 | Cu̯r̥C > CurC | Resyllabification |
| 11 | #pr > #br and #tr > #Tr | Word-initial cluster asymmetry |
| 12 | ow > ū | Back closing diphthong monophthongization |
| 13 | ej > ī | Front closing diphthong monophthongization; mirrors rule 12 |

### Open architecture

Crotonian+ covers the PIE-to-donor-language derivational layer only. The following areas remain unspecified and will require explicit decisions before the donor language can generate a full loanword lexicon for Ghandwa:

- **Vowel system:** Treatment of *a/o* distinction; merger or preservation; long vowel outcomes.
- **Labiovelar fate:** Whether *kʷ, gʷ* are preserved, delabialized, or merge with plain velars in specific environments. Garnier preserves them through most of his derivations without committing to a final outcome. Proto-Anatolian and Proto-Lydian labiovelar outcomes will be surveyed as candidate models for Crotonian+ resolution.
- **Word-final consonant behavior:** Cluster simplification, final *-m*, *-s*, *-r* outcomes.
- **Nominal and verbal morphology:** Stem classes, ending inventory. Nordwestblock suffix inventory (*-alo-*, *-ilo-*, *-ono-*, *-apa-*) is a candidate source for the donor's derivational morphology visible in place names and personal names entering Ghandwa.
- **Contact chronology:** Borrowings are assumed to occur after both languages have completed their treatment of PIE aspirates — Ghandwa's DʰR > voiced fricative series and the donor's rules 3–4. This means the diagnostic contrast (Ghandwa voiced fricative vs. donor voiceless/plain stop) is fully visible in loanword shape and does not require appeals to borrowing at different developmental stages.

Related substrate hypotheses for comparison:
- **Temematic** (Holzer 1989): centum + Dʰ > T + T > D; BSl contact zone. Shares devoicing rule with Crotonian; adds voicing of plain tenues (typologically suspect; not adopted in Crotonian+).
- **Ausonian** (Szemerényi 1991): centum + Dʰ > T; Italic substrate. Functionally a subset of Crotonian with less architecture.
- **Nordwestblock** (Kuhn 1962 and successors): Rhine-Weser area; no reconstructed phonological rule set; useful for suffix morphology and hydronymic root inventory in donor name material.
