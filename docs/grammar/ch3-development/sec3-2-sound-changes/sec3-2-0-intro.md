# §3.2 Regular Sound Changes

The sound changes that produce Ghandwa from PIE are grouped in three stages: a pre-stage of developments shared by all centum languages, a first stage of changes shared broadly across the western IE branches (laryngeal elimination, Osthoff's Law, and related developments), and a second stage of Ghandwa-specific innovations. Within each stage, changes are presented partly in chronological order and partly grouped thematically where interaction is the primary issue.

Relative chronology is recoverable in most cases and is argued explicitly where it is non-trivial. In the ordering summary (§3.2.4) the full rule sequence is collected for reference.

Three rules function as **standing constraints** rather than diachronic changes: they apply after every stage and reapply until convergence. These are described in their diachronic context in §3.2.1 (Boukólos rule and labiovelar absorption) and given their authoritative synchronic treatment in §4.2 (*sec4-2-phonology/sec4-2-phonology.md*).

Readers primarily interested in the large-scale phonological differences between PIE and Ghandwa should read at minimum §3.2.1 (labiovelar developments), §3.2.2.3 (laryngeal loss), §3.2.3.1 (syllabic resonant vocalization), and §3.2.3.3 (aspirate fricativization).
