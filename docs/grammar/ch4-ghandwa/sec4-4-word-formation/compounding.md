## Compositional Stems

### ā-stem Compositional Stems

In compounds, ā-stems appear with the bare stem, dropping the citation long vowel. The compositional first member is the stem without final *-ā*:

| Citation form | Compositional form |
|---|---|
| *akʷā* 'water, river' | *akʷa- / akʷ-* |

Example: *akʷa-* + *ekʷos* → *akʷekʷos* 'river-horse'. No hiatus rule is needed; the final short *-a* of the compositional stem and the vowel-initial second member meet without special treatment.

### Glide Insertion Before Vowel-Initial Members

When a compositional first member ends in *-i* before a vowel-initial second member, the *-i* surfaces as *-j-* by the general hiatus glide insertion rule (§5.2 of the phonological history). No special compounding rule is needed; this is a regular phonological process.

| Compositional form | Second member | Compound |
|---|---|---|
| *mori-* 'sea, marine' | *ekʷos* 'horse' | *morjekʷos* 'sea-horse' |

*mori-* is the compositional form of the marine/sea first member. Before consonant-initial second members it would appear as *mori-*; before vowel-initial members the glide insertion rule applies automatically.

## Attested Compounds

| Compound | Gloss | Structure |
|---|---|---|
| *akʷekʷos* | river-horse; water-horse; hippopotamus | *akʷa-* + *ekʷos* |
| *morjekʷos* | sea-horse; horse-headed sea monster | *morj-* + *ekʷos* |
| *ekʷelos* | little horse, foal | *ekʷos* + *-el-* (dim.) |
| *akʷekʷelos* | little river-horse; young hippopotamus | *akʷa-* + *ekʷelos* |
| *morjekʷelos* | little sea-horse; seahorse (fish) | *morj-* + *ekʷelos* |
| *meguɣoistós* | great-monster; kaiju-type being | *megu-* + *ɣoistós* |

## Compound Accentuation

The second element retains its inherent accent; any accent on the first element is suppressed. Inherited from PIE compound accentuation. See `docs/grammar/ch4-ghandwa/paradigms-nominal.md` §Compound Accentuation for worked examples.

---

## Compound vs. NP Phrase

A noun + adjective syntactic phrase (§4.5) differs from a lexicalized compound:

- Syntactic phrase: *ɣoistós megus* 'great monster' — a large instance of the class *ɣoistós*
- Compound: *meguɣoistós* 'great-monster' — a lexicalized class term (kaiju-type being)

The compound form *meguɣoistós* is a class noun, not a modifier+head phrase. It follows the qualifying-first-member-before-head pattern common in PIE and its daughters.
