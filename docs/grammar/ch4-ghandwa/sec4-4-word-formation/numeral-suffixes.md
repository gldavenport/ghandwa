# Ghandwa Dictionary Notes: *-dékes* and *-dékā*

Two late analogical suffixes forming decimal numerals ("X tens"). Both are sub-formal in origin and attest primarily in colloquial, regional, and speech-colored written registers. They are in free variation or regional complementary distribution, reflecting a shared derivational source rather than a functional opposition.

---

## Etymology

Both suffixes derive from a secondary nominal stem abstracted from the inherited numeral **\*dekám** "ten" (< PIE \*deḱm̥(t)).

The numeral was reanalyzed as an athematic accusative singular: **\*dek-am**, where **\*-am** is the expected athematic acc.sg. ending. This yields a back-formed athematic nominative singular **\*déks** "a unit of ten, decad," declined as a root noun:

| Case | Form |
|---|---|
| nom.sg | \*déks |
| acc.sg | \*dekám |
| gen.sg | \*dekés |
| nom.pl | \*dékes |

From the nom.pl. **\*dékes** "tens, units of ten" comes the suffixal element **\*-dékes**. From a parallel neuter plural or collective formation **\*dékā** "tens, groups of ten" comes **\*-dékā**. The collective reading of **\*-dékā** makes it slightly more classifier-like; in practice the two are interchangeable, their distribution conditioned by dialect and register rather than by any stable semantic contrast.

---

## *-dékes*

**\*-dékes** *suffixal numeral element; late/regional Ghandwa*

Morphologically the nom.pl. of the root noun **\*déks**. Forms decimal numerals by compounding with the lower numeral.

| Form | Gloss |
|---|---|
| \*duwo-dékes | "twenty" |
| \*treie-dékes | "thirty" |
| \*kʷetur-dékes | "forty" |
| \*penkʷe-dékes | "fifty" |
| \*seks-dékes | "sixty" |
| \*septam-dékes | "seventy" |
| \*oktow-dékes | "eighty" |
| \*néwan-dékes | "ninety" |

---

## *-dékā*

**\*-dékā** *suffixal numeral element; late/regional Ghandwa*

Morphologically a neuter plural or collective of the secondary stem **\*dek-**. Functionally parallel to **\*-dékes**; see above on their distribution.

| Form | Gloss |
|---|---|
| \*duwo-dékā | "twenty" |
| \*treie-dékā | "thirty" |
| \*kʷetur-dékā | "forty" |
| \*penkʷe-dékā | "fifty" |
| \*seks-dékā | "sixty" |
| \*septam-dékā | "seventy" |
| \*oktow-dékā | "eighty" |
| \*néwan-dékā | "ninety" |

## Contrast with inherited standard forms

The analogical forms contrast with the inherited formal system built on **\*-kamtā**:

| Value | Inherited | Analogical (*-dékes*) | Analogical (*-dékā*) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 20 | \*wīkamtī | \*duwo-dékes | \*duwo-dékā |
| 30 | \*trīkamtā | \*treie-dékes | \*treie-dékā |
| 40 | \*kʷeturkamtā | \*kʷetur-dékes | \*kʷetur-dékā |
| 50 | \*penkʷēkamtā | \*penkʷe-dékes | \*penkʷe-dékā |
| 60 | \*sekskamtā | \*seks-dékes | \*seks-dékā |
| 70 | \*septamkamtā | \*septam-dékes | \*septam-dékā |
| 80 | \*oktōkamtā | \*oktow-dékes | \*oktow-dékā |
| 90 | \*néwankamtā | \*néwan-dékes | \*néwan-dékā |

The inherited **\*wīkamtī** for 20 reflects the dual; the analogical forms generalize the plural stem throughout.
