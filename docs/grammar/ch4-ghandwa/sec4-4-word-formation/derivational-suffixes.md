## *-el-* Diminutive

**Source:** Inherited directly from PIE \*-elo-. Formally identical with the PIE suffix; no Ghandwa innovation.

**Comparanda:** Greek *-ελος* (e.g. diminutive and hypocoristic formations); Latin *-ulus* < \*-olo-* — a divergent Italic reflex showing vowel lowering *e→o* followed by rhotacism in some environments. The Latin reflex confirms the inherited status of the suffix while showing that Ghandwa *-el-* is not an independent innovation.

**Thematic forms:**

| Form | Gender/class | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| *-elos* | animate/common (thematic masc.) | |
| *-elom* | neuter (thematic neut.) | |
| *-elā* | feminine (ā-stem) | |

**Attachment:** The suffix attaches directly to the nominal stem. No intervening vowel.

**Productivity:** Regular and productive.

**Examples:**

| Base | Diminutive | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| *ekʷos* 'horse' | *ekʷelos* | little horse, foal |
| *morjekʷos* 'sea-horse' | *morjekʷelos* | little sea-horse, seahorse (fish) |
| *akʷekʷos* 'river-horse' | *akʷekʷelos* | little river-horse, young hippopotamus |

## Instrument Suffixes: *-trom*, *-ðrom*, *-klom*, *-ɣlom*

**Source:** Inherited from PIE \*-tro-m, \*-dʰro-m, \*-tlo-m, \*-dʰlo-m. Ghandwa outcomes via regular sound changes: \*dʰ → ð (§3.2.3.3); \*tl → kl, \*ðl → ɣl (§3.2.3.7).

**Paradigm:** Neuter o-stem throughout.

| PIE source | Ghandwa | Function |
|---|---|---|
| \*-tro-m | *-trom* | plain; tool, means, or result of verbal action |
| \*-dʰro-m | *-ðrom* | marked; intensive or specialized instrument |
| \*-tlo-m | *-klom* | plain (lateral series); tool, means, or result |
| \*-dʰlo-m | *-ɣlom* | marked (lateral series); intensive or specialized |

**Structural parallel.** The stop-series pair (*-trom* / *-ðrom*) and the lateral-series pair (*-klom* / *-ɣlom*) both instantiate the same Ghandwa morphological pattern: a plain member with neutral valency alongside a fricative/marked member with intensive or lexicalized function. The same pattern appears in the agentive nt-stems (*-gʷonts* / *-ghwonts*, § below).

**Productivity.** The plain members (*-trom*, *-klom*) are more freely applicable; the marked members (*-ðrom*, *-ɣlom*) tend toward fixed lexicalized items.

## Agentive nt-Stem Suffix: *-onts ~ -antés*

**Source:** Inherited from PIE \*-ónts ~ \*-n̥tés, the active participial nt-stem formation. Lexicalized in Ghandwa as a productive agentive nominal suffix.

**Paradigm:** nt-stem (animate). Nominative *-onts*, oblique stem *-ant-* (via \*n̥ → an, §3.2.3.1). The nominative ending *-nts* is preserved unchanged — cf. *dónts ~ dantés* 'tooth'.

| Form | Ending | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| nom.sg. | *-onts* | < \*-ónts |
| gen.sg. | *-antés* | < \*-n̥tés, syllabic resonant vocalization |
| stem | *-ant-* | extracted oblique base |

**Semantic range.** Forms agent nouns from verbal roots. When combining with labiovelar-initial roots, the suffix participates in a Ghandwa-level derivational doublet:

- *-gʷonts* ~ *-gʷantés*: 'grazer, feeder' (neutral/ecological; < \*gʷeh₃-)
- *-ghwonts* ~ *-ghwantés*: 'gulper, swallower, devourer' (marked/predatory; < \*h₁gʷʰ-)

This doublet is a Ghandwa-level derivational pair, not a daughter-language innovation.

**Productivity.** Productive as a compound-final agentive element; attested as a free noun in simplex formations.
