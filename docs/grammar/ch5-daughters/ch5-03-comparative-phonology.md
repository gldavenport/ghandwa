### 2.2 Stage 2: Early Divergent Western / Central / Eastern Responses

This is the main age of regional fragmentation and the most Greek-like phase in the overall comparison set. Its main characteristics are: no single uncontested supraregional standard; strong regional divergence; continuing but uneven contact between regions; island, delta, riverine, and hill-zone differentiation; and the beginning of durable daughter-language identities.

During this stage, innovations spread through contact networks rather than along a perfectly clean family-tree pattern. Major regions diverge first; finer city-by-city diversification is secondary.

The core idea is that the same inherited instabilities are resolved in **different ways** across Western, Central, and Eastern. This is an era of rapid change under poor standardization — language change moves fast when there is no educational infrastructure or literary norm to slow it down.

#### Stage 2 matrix

| Domain | Western (A) | Central (B) | Eastern (C) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **2.1 Stress** | **initial stress** | **bounded nonfinal stress**, tending penultimate | **initial stress** |
| **2.2 Non-sibilant fricatives** | voiced fricatives resolve by **devoicing** everywhere | voiced fricatives resolve **by environment**: position-sensitive hardening, gliding, rhotacism, and devoicing | voiced fricatives resolve by **hardening** everywhere |
| **2.3 /z/ and /s/** | /z/ **devoices** to /s/ (part of general devoicing) | inherited /z/ **preserved**; voicing rule not productive; /z/ remains as systemic orphan | /s/ **voices at word edges** (s > z / V\_#, N\_#, R\_#); **z > d / N\_**; /z/ otherwise survives massively expanded |
| **2.4 Labiovelars** | labiovelars **delabialize** | labiovelars reduce only in **conditioned environments** | labiovelars **develarize** to labials |

Western and Eastern both innovating initial stress is understood as **convergent, not shared** — an areal feature arising independently through contact in a dialect continuum, not a shared innovation implying a subgroup.

---

#### 2A (Western) current formulation

- **stress > initial**
- **β > ɸ**
- **ð > θ**
- **ɣ > x**
- **ɣʷ > xʷ** (in practice often treated in running tests as leading to **x**)
- **z > s** (part of general devoicing; eliminates /z/ entirely)
- **kʷ > k**
- **gʷ > g**
- **cluster spirantization before s** (not before t):
  - **ks > xs**
  - **ps > ɸs**

Note: Western exits Stage 2 with a large voiceless fricative inventory (ɸ θ x from devoicing, plus ɸ x from cluster spirantization) with no voiced counterparts — a systemic instability that drives Stage 3+ developments (see Appendix II).

---

#### 2B (Central) current formulation

Central is explicitly the **slower, intermediate branch** — its voiced fricative outcomes are more varied and environment-sensitive than either Western (wholesale devoicing) or Eastern (wholesale hardening). This makes Central the "messiest" of the three branches, with different reflexes conditioned by position, flanking segments, and place of articulation.

##### Stress
- **bounded nonfinal stress**, tending penultimate
- Central does not innovate initial stress; it retains the Stage 1 prosodic system with a gradual penultimate default

##### Non-sibilant voiced fricatives

Resolved by a six-step ordered pipeline (excluding /z/, which is handled separately). Rules are ordered; each bleeds subsequent rules for already-resolved segments.

**2B.1 Homorganic nasal assimilation** (bleeds 2B.2–2B.6)
- Vβm → Vmm (β fully assimilates to following m; geminate retained)
- Vðn → Vnn (ð fully assimilates to following n; geminate retained)
- VɣʷmV → VwmV (ɣʷ partially assimilates: labial feature wins, no gemination; requires flanking vowels)
- VɣnV: no place overlap; ɣ falls through to post-nasal hardening

**2B.2 Post-nasal hardening** (bleeds 2B.3–2B.6)
- β ð ɣ ɣʷ → b d g gʷ / N\_
- Cross-linguistically the most robust hardening environment; nasals are [-continuant] and spread manner rightward.

**2B.3 Word-initial pre-liquid hardening** (bleeds 2B.4–2B.6)
- β ð ɣ → b d g / #\_R
- Onset cluster fortition: initial position is a strong position; stop+liquid onsets are typologically preferred over fricative+liquid onsets.
- Attestational parallel: PIt *frāter*, PG *brōþēr* for β; analogous for ɣ and ð.
- ɣʷ in this environment: vacuous in current lexicon; specified as ɣʷ → gʷ for completeness.

**2B.4 β and ɣʷ gliding** (bleeds 2B.5–2B.6 for these segments)
- β → w / \_V (prevocalic; all environments after 2B.1–2B.3)
- β → w / \_C (pre-consonantal; vacuous in current lexicon after 2B.1–2B.3 resolve attested cases)
- ɣʷ → w / \_V (prevocalic; all environments)
- β word-finally: unspecified; no attested cases in current lexicon. Flag for review.
- ɣʷ in \_C: vacuous in current lexicon; falls to default devoicing (2B.6) as xʷ if encountered.
- Typological motivation: β and ɣʷ both carry a labial feature; its natural resolution in weak positions is the labial glide w.

**2B.5 ð and ɣ in sonorant-flanked environment**
- ð → r / {V,R}\_V (rhotacism: after vowel or liquid, before vowel)
- ɣ → w / {V,R}\_V (gliding: after vowel or liquid, before vowel)
- Liquids are transparent: R\_V patterns with V\_V. The following vowel is the dominant conditioning factor; confirmed by lexicon mining (all attested R\_ cases for ð and ɣ are R\_V; R\_C and R\_# are vacuous).
- Lexicon examples: *melðoː* (lð → lr), *βarðos* (rð → rr), *βargus* (rɣ → rw), *margus* (rɣ → rw).
- ɣ → w (not j): ɣ is a back/velar segment; its natural weakening trajectory is toward a back glide, not a front glide.

**2B.6 Default devoicing** (elsewhere)
- ð → θ (covers: #\_V, \_C, word-final)
- ɣ → x (covers: #\_V, \_C, word-final)
- ɣʷ → xʷ (vacuous in current lexicon; retained as fallback)
- β is fully resolved by 2B.4 and should not appear here.
- Word-final voiced fricatives: unattested in current lexicon; unspecified. Flag any word-final β ð ɣ ɣʷ in new entries for review.

**2B.7 Labiovelar dissimilation** (OCP-[+round])
- kʷ gʷ xʷ → k g x / when adjacent syllable contains {kʷ, gʷ, xʷ, w}
- /w/ is immune as a target; it is only a trigger.
- Direction: rightward (first syllable wins, second loses).

##### Rule interaction summary

Post-nasal hardening (2B.2) bleeds all later rules for nasally-preceded segments. Word-initial pre-liquid hardening (2B.3) bleeds 2B.4, ensuring #βr → br (not wr): onset fortition takes precedence over prevocalic gliding. The β/ɣʷ gliding rule (2B.4) bleeds the sonorant-flanked rule (2B.5) and default devoicing (2B.6) for β and ɣʷ. Remaining ð and ɣ resolve by environment in 2B.5 then 2B.6.

##### Comparison with sister branches

Central's system contrasts sharply with Western (wholesale devoicing: β→ɸ, ð→θ, ɣ→x everywhere) and Eastern (wholesale hardening: β→b, ð→d, ɣ→g, ɣʷ→gʷ everywhere including V\_V). Central's position-sensitive system — hardening in strong positions (post-nasal, word-initial pre-liquid), gliding/rhotacism in voiced flanking environments, devoicing elsewhere — is typologically the most phonetically naturalistic of the three, sitting between systemic uniformity and pure phonetic pressure.

Contrast from the same lexical items:
- *βargus* (high): Western ɸargus — Central wargus — Eastern bargus
- *βarðos* (beard): Western ɸarθos — Central warrðos → warros — Eastern bardos
- *melðoː* (hammer name): Western melθoː — Central melroː — Eastern meldoː

##### /z/: inherited, preserved, orphaned
/z/ is excluded from the non-sibilant pipeline above. Central preserves Ghandwa's inherited /z/ inventory (~55 lemmas) without change. The voicing rule that created /z/ in Ghandwa is **not productive** in Central — no new VsV contacts produce /z/.

After the non-sibilant pipeline eliminates β ð ɣ ɣʷ, /z/ is left as the **sole surviving voiced fricative** in Central — a systemic orphan. This instability drives varied Stage 3 outcomes by environment (see §3, 3B).

##### Labiovelars
- Reduced only in **specific conditioned environments**
- Exact triggers not yet fully settled (see Appendix I.B)

#### 2C (Eastern) current formulation

- **stress > initial**
- **s-voicing extends to word edges** (while voiced fricatives are still present in the system):
  - **s > z / V\_#** — word-final /s/ voices after vowels
  - **s > z / N\_#** — word-final /s/ voices after nasals (feeds post-nasal hardening: -Vns > -Vnz > -Vnd)
  - **s > z / R\_#** — word-final /s/ voices after liquids (feeds rhotacism at Stage 3: -VRs > -VRz > -VRr > -Vr)
  - Note: word-initial #s > z / \_[+voiced] was considered and dropped — initial position is a fortition environment, not a lenition one; vacuous in practice.
- **voiced fricatives harden everywhere:**
  - **β > b, ð > d, ɣ > g, ɣʷ > gʷ** (all positions including intervocalic; contrast Central where V\_V gives gliding/rhotacism)
  - **z > d / N\_** — post-nasal /z/ hardens to stop (the one environment where the sibilant participates in hardening)
  - /z/ is otherwise **not subject to hardening**; survives massively expanded
- **kʷ > p**
- **gʷ > b**

The ordering matters: s-voicing applies while the system still has active voiced fricatives. Fortition then hardens all non-sibilant fricatives to stops in a single unconditional pass. The result is a massive /z/ inventory — inherited intervocalic /z/ plus new word-final /z/ — which drives later rhotacism (see §3, 3C).

#### Summary of Stage 2

```text
Stage 2 (Dark Ages) — rapid change under poor standardization

2A (Western)
  1. stress > initial
  2. β ð ɣ ɣʷ > ɸ θ x xʷ (wholesale devoicing)
  3. z > s (general devoicing)
  4. kʷ gʷ > k g
  5. cluster spirantization before s: ks > xs, ps > ɸs
  → exits with large voiceless fricative inventory (time bomb)

2B (Central)
  1. bounded nonfinal stress, tending penultimate
  2. non-sibilant voiced fricatives resolve by ordered pipeline:
     2B.1  Vβm > Vmm, Vðn > Vnn, VɣʷmV > VwmV  (homorganic nasal assimilation)
     2B.2  β ð ɣ ɣʷ > b d g gʷ / N_            (post-nasal hardening)
     2B.3  β ð ɣ (ɣʷ) > b d g (gʷ) / #_R       (word-initial pre-liquid hardening)
     2B.4  β > w / _V,_C; ɣʷ > w / _V           (β/ɣʷ gliding)
     2B.5  ð > r, ɣ > w / {V,R}_V               (sonorant-flanked rhotacism/gliding)
     2B.6  ð > θ, ɣ > x / elsewhere             (default devoicing)
     2B.7  OCP-[+round] labiovelar dissimilation
  3. /z/ preserved; voicing rule not productive; orphaned as sole voiced fricative
  4. labiovelars reduce only under conditioned environments

2C (Eastern)
  1. stress > initial
  2. s-voicing at word edges:
     s > z / V_#, N_#, R_#
     (#s > z / _[+voiced] considered and dropped)
  3. voiced fricative hardening (unconditional):
     β ð ɣ ɣʷ > b d g gʷ (everywhere, including V_V)
     z > d / N_ (post-nasal only)
     z elsewhere not subject to hardening
  4. kʷ gʷ > p b
  → exits with massive /z/ inventory (time bomb)
```

## 3. Further Divergence

### 3.1 Stage 3: Branch-Specific Developments (Dawn Age)

Stage 3 is the first real recovery phase. It is not the return of Stage 0, but a new order growing out of the post-collapse dialect world.

Three sociolinguistic layers are to be distinguished:

1. **Neo-Ghandwa** at the top, primarily **priestly-scholarly** in status;
2. beneath it, a practical **merchant-warrior koine** serving trade, movement, and interregional contact;
3. beneath that, the **common tongues**, i.e. the ordinary daughter vernaculars.

Neo-Ghandwa is not the continuous survival of old Ghandwa. It is a later learned recovery built from fragmentary evidence. Because the surviving corpus is narrow and skewed (see ch5-01), Neo-Ghandwa is expected to be formulaic, conservative in ideology, only partly authentic to Stage 0 speech, and disproportionately influenced by priestly, ritual, legal, and inscriptional language.

At the same time, some daughter vernaculars begin to acquire their own local literary or scribal norms.

This is the stage where the daughters no longer simply respond differently to the same pressures, but begin developing branch-specific histories.

#### 3A (Western) current direction

Current landed decisions:

1. **later loss/simplification of x, ɸ in some Stage 2 spirantization clusters with compensatory lengthening**
   - not necessarily fully general or immediate
   - example: **kréps > kreɸs > kreːs** (possible later development)

2. **no rhotacism** (no /z/ to rhotacize — already devoiced to /s/ at Stage 2)

3. **voiceless fricative instability** as the main long-term pressure. Western exits Stage 2 with ɸ, θ, x (xʷ) from devoicing and additional ɸ, x from cluster spirantization — a large voiceless fricative inventory with no voiced counterparts. Expected resolutions include ɸ → f, θ → t or s, x → h. Different Western sub-branches may resolve different fricatives differently or at different rates.

#### 3B (Central) current direction

Central remains the least fixed, but the following broad profile is now in place:

1. **/z/ resolves by environment with varied outcomes.** After the non-sibilant pipeline eliminates β ð ɣ ɣʷ, /z/ is the sole surviving voiced fricative — a systemic orphan that must resolve. Resolution is **not uniform**: different environments (intervocalic, pre-consonantal, post-liquid) are expected to produce different outcomes. Partial rhotacism (z → r in some environments) is likely but not yet specified. Different Central sub-branches may also diverge in their /z/ outcomes.

2. **bridge-zone mergers and mixed local reflexes develop**

#### 3C (Eastern) current direction

Current landed decisions:

1. **rhotacism** as the expected main resolution of Eastern's massive /z/ inventory, with environment-specific outcomes. The morphological consequences are dramatic: inherited case endings in -Vs surface as -Vz after Stage 2 edge-voicing, then as -Vr after rhotacism — radically altering the right edge (cf. West Germanic, Old Norse).

2. **final-vowel shortening and later apocope**
3. **right-edge morphological erosion**

#### Current z-resolution map for Eastern (C)

Committed outcomes:

- **#zV- > #dV-** — word-initial z hardens to stop (fortition in absolute initial position)
- **-Nz-, -Nz# > -Nd-, -Nd#** — post-nasal z hardens to stop (already resolved at Stage 2 via z > d / N\_)
- **-VzV- > -VrV-** — intervocalic rhotacism (core outcome)
- **-Vz# > -Vr#** — word-final post-vocalic z rhotacizes
- **-RzV- > -RRV-** — post-liquid z assimilates to preceding liquid (gemination)
- **-VRs# > -VRz# > -VRr# > -Vr#** — word-final post-liquid s voices, rhotacizes, then cluster simplifies

Still unresolved:
- **-VzC-**: outcome unknown
- **-RzC-**: outcome unknown
- Whether #zV- > #dV- is a separate rule or patterns with post-nasal as general fortition

Eastern is expected to sound heavier and more clipped at the right edge than Western.

### 3.2 Stage 4: Flourishing / Stabilized Standards

By Stage 4, strong literary, scribal, and educational traditions exist. Language change in speech continues normally, but visible high-register change slows because standards become entrenched.

- conservative writing systems stabilize
- schools and learned traditions preserve older norms
- canonical texts constrain elite usage
- the difference between written prestige forms and everyday speech becomes more socially significant

Stage 4 and sub-daughter developments are covered in per-daughter works, not this grammar volume.
