## Appendix I: Open Questions

Items here are unresolved and require active decisions before being promoted to §§2–4.

### I.A /z/ and /s/ across the three branches

The three-branch framework for /z/ is decided:

- **Western (A):** /z/ devoices to /s/ as part of general devoicing. No /z/ survives. Resolved.
- **Central (B):** inherited /z/ preserved from Ghandwa (~55 lemmas); voicing rule not productive. /z/ is orphaned as sole voiced fricative after non-sibilant pipeline. Resolves at Stage 3 with **varied outcomes by environment** — partial rhotacism likely but not specified. Different Central sub-branches may diverge.
- **Eastern (C):** /z/ survives Stage 2 stop-hardening (sibilants not subject to hardening) except post-nasally, where z > d. /s/ voicing **extends to word edges** (s > z / V\_#, N\_#, R\_#; #s > z / \_[+voiced] considered and dropped), massively expanding /z/ inventory. Post-nasal /z/ hardens to /d/; post-liquid final /z/ rhotacizes then simplifies (-VRz > -VRr > -Vr). Resolves at Stage 3 with rhotacism as the main outcome.

#### Remaining open questions

1. **Eastern #zV- > #dV-:** is initial fortition a separate rule, or does initial position pattern with post-nasal as a general "strong environment" for hardening?
2. **Eastern -RzV- > -RRV-:** do the resulting geminates become a new phonological category in Eastern, or do they simplify later?
3. **Eastern -VzC- and -RzC-:** outcome unknown for pre-consonantal /z/ in Eastern.
4. **Central /z/ outcomes by environment:** what happens to intervocalic /z/ (~40 lemmas), pre-consonantal /z/ (11 lemmas), and post-liquid /z/ (8 lemmas)?
5. **Sub-branch variation:** how much do Central and Eastern sub-branches diverge in their /z/ outcomes?

#### Lexicon inventory (~55 lemmas with /z/)

Retained for reference. This inventory applies directly to Central (which preserves it unchanged) and indirectly to Eastern (which adds to it via edge-voicing).

**Not attested in Ghandwa:** word-initial (#z), post-nasal (Nz), word-final (z#), z + voiceless consonant. Eastern's edge-voicing creates new word-initial, word-final, post-nasal, and post-liquid-final /z/ that Ghandwa did not have.

**Intervocalic (VzV) — ~40 lemmas.** Dominated by *vezu-* compounds and *-oizos* / *-ezos* suffixal elements. Core vocabulary includes:

- *vezús* 'good' (e\_z\_u)
- *ézar* 'blood' (e\_z\_a)
- *nā́zā* 'nose' (a\_z\_a)
- *ɣoizós* 'spear' (i\_z\_o)
- *lóizā* 'furrow, track' (i\_z\_a)
- *vézonts* 'bison' (e\_z\_o)
- *svézōr* 'sister' (e\_z\_o)
- *álizos* 'alder' (i\_z\_o)
- *auzantóm* 'gold' (u\_z\_a)

**Pre-consonantal (VzC) — 11 lemmas.** All clusters are z + voiced consonant:

- zd: *nizdós* 'nest', *ózdos* 'branch', *sízdeti* 'be sitting'
- zl: *kózlos* 'hazel', *nā́zlos* 'nostril'
- zr: *vózrā* 'springtime', *vózrī* 'Spring (personification)'
- zn: *ðúznos* 'light brown', *úznos* 'onion'
- zv: *ðúzvos* 'dark', *duzvirós* 'ne'er-do-well'

**Post-liquid (Rz) — 8 lemmas:**

- rz: *órzos* 'arse', *ðarzús* 'bold', *tarzús* 'dry'
- lz: *palzā́* 'cliff', *pálzā* 'woman's dress', *palzóm* 'boulder'
- rz(+n): *pérznā* 'heel', *karznóm* 'top of skull'

## Appendix II: Future Phonological Domains

The current Stage 2 changes are almost entirely consonantal. Real dialectal drift into unintelligibility requires vowel changes and additional innovations. Prioritized by impact:

**Non-negotiable** (without these, divergence stays superficial):

1. **Unstressed vowel reduction and syncope.** Initial stress in Western and Eastern weakens non-initial syllables. Reduction to schwa? Full syncope of medials? Only in open syllables? Central with penultimate stress would erode different positions. Probably the single biggest missing piece.

2. **Vowel mergers and shifts.** The five-vowel system (i e a o u, each long and short) is ripe for partial merger or chain shift. Natural candidates: a/o merger in some daughter; ē/ī or ō/ū merger; diphthongization of long vowels (ē → ie, ō → uo); monophthongization of old diphthongs (ei → ē, ou → ō). Could split three ways.

**High impact:**

3. **Palatalization.** The most common consonant innovation in IE daughter histories. Natural triggers: k/g → palatals or affricates before front vowels; t/d → affricates before j/i; s → ʃ before i/j. Interacts with vowel changes.

**Secondary / flavor:**

4. **Voiceless fricative instability in Western.** Western has ɸ, θ, x with no voiced counterparts — typologically unstable. Natural outcomes: ɸ → f, θ → t or s, x → h.

5. **Nasal vowel development.** VN → Ṽ if any daughter loses nasals before fricatives or in codas.

6. **Hiatus resolution.** Syncope and consonant loss create new vowel-vowel contacts. Daughters would resolve them differently.

7. **Final consonant changes.** Final devoicing, final fricative weakening, final stop loss — interacts with Eastern's right-edge morphological erosion.

## Appendix V: CS / Dialect / -ln- Decisions (2026-03-01)

Source: ChatGPT session export, 2026-03-01. Early session point decisions; some may already be reflected in the Western/Central/Eastern framework or Appendix IV; others not yet captured elsewhere.

- Dialectal outcomes for *-ln-*: West: -ln- > -ll-; East: -ln- > -nn-; Central (C): -Vln- > -Vwn-.

- Dialect geography model: innovations spread inward from the edges; the center becomes a mixed-isogloss zone; later, edge dialects also develop incomplete isoglosses and split into subdialects.

- CS status/label: CS is treated as a codified high register (a supradialectal, normed variety), not a purely geographic dialect.

- CS phonological profile (vowels): CS retains vowel 'shape' conservatively (keeps two-part vocalism such as V+glide sequences, even if component qualities drift).

- CS phonological profile (glides): CS retains glides and can acquire additional glides in ways other dialects do not.

- CS glide-creation (morphology example): for s-stem-like material, genitive -ezos > -ejos; hypothetical -oze- > -owe- (environment-driven z > j / z > w).

- Representation choice: all IPA diphthongs were replaced with Vj/Vw pairs (e.g., oi > oj; ei > ej) as the working representation.

- Design stance on interactions: any knock-on interactions from the Vj/Vw rewrite are acceptable; rule ordering/rules will be adjusted if outputs are undesirable.
