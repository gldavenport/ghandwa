# Sources Used for Italic–Germanic Verbal-System Conclusions

Date: 2026-05-16

This source list documents the archive sources used or relied on in the chat discussion that produced the Ghandwa verbal-system handoff.

## Core source categories

### 1. Branch-level grammatical surveys

These were used for high-level verbal-system comparison: present systems, inherited PIE endings, stem classes, preterite/perfect developments, participles, non-finites, and voice categories.

| Source / source group | Used for |
|---|---|
| `hchiel` | Branch-level surveys for Italic, Germanic, Celtic, and Indo-European verbal morphology. Used for broad comparison of verbal categories and daughter-branch restructuring. |
| `fortson-2010-indo-european-language-culture` | General IE and daughter-branch morphology, including PIE verbal categories and how they are reflected in major branches. |
| `beekes-2011-comparative-ie-linguistics-markdown` | General PIE verbal morphology and comparative daughter evidence. |
| `kapovic-ed-2017-ie-languages-2nd` | Handbook-level branch morphology and diachronic comparison. |
| `olander-ed-2022-ie-language-family` | Up-to-date branch-level Indo-European family reference; used as a general comparative guardrail. |
| `woodard-ed-2008-ancient-languages-europe` | Branch description support, especially for ancient Italic/Celtic/Germanic contexts. |

|---|---|
| `ringe-2017-pie-to-proto-germanic-2nd` | Main model for PIE-to-Proto-Germanic phonology/morphology and for thinking in staged transformation terms. Especially relevant for Germanic present classes, preterite development, and inherited vs. innovated categories. |
| `kroonen-2013-proto-germanic` | Proto-Germanic inherited vocabulary and verb-family evidence; used to cross-check roots, derived verbs, and shared inherited formations. |

| `walkden-2014-syntactic-reconstruction-proto-germanic` | Germanic syntactic reconstruction; secondary relevance for preverbs, non-finites, and verbal constructions. |

## Conclusions supported by these sources

The chat’s conclusions were based on the combined evidence above:

1. Italic and Germanic both preserve PIE-derived primary active present endings well enough for Ghandwa to use a conservative present active paradigm.
2. Thematic *-e/o-* presents are the safest default finite class.
3. Athematic/root presents should exist, but mainly as relics/high-frequency inherited verbs.
4. *-ye/o-* and *-éye/o-* formations were inherited but also continued as productive or semi-productive derivational mechanisms in daughter branches, especially visible in Germanic *jan*-verbs and Italic factitive/derived verb behavior.
5. Nasal presents are valid inherited material but should be subclassed and not overgeneralized.
6. Middle/deponent material is acceptable as relic material, but should not be central to a specifically Italic–Germanic Ghandwa core.
7. The past system is the least settled area: a broad preterite zone is plausible, but a fully Germanic-style weak dental preterite probably belongs more naturally to daughter development unless explicitly promoted into Ghandwa.
8. Celtic confirms much of the older WIE verbal inheritance but should not dictate the core because Celtic later reorganizes the verb much more heavily around preverbs, absolute/conjunct forms, verbal nouns, and r/deponent behavior.
