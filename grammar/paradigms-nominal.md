## ā-stem (feminine)

Example: *gvénā* "woman"

|     | sing-orth | sing-ipa | plur-orth | plur-ipa |
|-----|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|
| nom | -ā        | aː       | -ās       | aːs      |
| voc | -a        | a        | -ās       | aːs      |
| acc | -ām       | aːm      | -āns      | aːns     |
| gen | -ās       | aːs      | -ām       | aːm      |
| dat | -āi       | aːj      | -āmos     | aː.mos   |
| ins | -ā        | aː       | -āmis     | aː.mis   |
| loc | -āi       | aːj      | -āzu      | aː.zu    |

Notes:
- Dat/loc syncreted in the singular (-āi)
- Ins sg = bare lengthened stem vowel, parallel to o-stem ins sg -ō
- Voc sg -a: back-formed by analogy with o-stem voc sg -e (bare stem vowel)
- Gen/abl/loc sg: standard syncretism; abl = gen (-ās)

## o-stem (masculine)

Example: *viros* "man"

|     | sing-orth | sing-ipa | plur-orth | plur-ipa |
|-----|-----------|----------|-----------|----------|
| nom | -os       | os       | -ōs       | oːs      |
| voc | -e        | e        | -ōs       | oːs      |
| acc | -om       | om       | -ons      | ons      |
| gen | -ozio     | o.z͡jo   | -ōm       | oːm      |
| dat | -ōi       | oːj      | -oimos    | oj.mos   |
| ins | -ō        | oː       | -ōis      | oːjs     |
| loc | -oi       | oj       | -oizu     | oj.zu    |

Notes:
- Gen sg -ozio: conservative PIE *-osyo; contrast Latin -ī, Germanic -az/-es (both innovations)
- Dat sg -ōi: PIE *-ōy, final y → i spelling
- Ins sg -ō: PIE *-oh₁, laryngeal lost leaving long vowel
- Dat/ins distinct in the singular (unlike ā-stem)
- Loc pl -oizu / ā-stem loc pl -āzu: -zu element consistent across stem classes
- Dat pl -oimos / ā-stem dat pl -āmos: -mos element consistent across stem classes
- Ins pl -ōis: PIE *-ōys
- /z͡j/ in gen sg is typologically unstable; ripe for daughter-level change

Notes:
- Gen sg -ezos: from PIE *-es-os; oblique stem *-ez-* throughout (PIE *-es-* → *-ez-* by intervocalic s→z voicing)
- Nom/acc/voc pl *-ā*: from PIE *-h₂, parallel to o-stem and u-stem neuter plurals
- Voc sg *-e*: bare stem vowel, parallel to o-stem masc voc sg

## Compound Accentuation

In compounds, the second element retains its inherent accent; any accent on the first element is suppressed. This is inherited from PIE compound accentuation.

Examples:
- *angleβorós* 'ember-bearer': *ángle-* (from *óngvōl*) loses accent; *βorós* 'bearer' keeps its suffix accent
- *ognologós* 'ash-gatherer': *ógno-* (from *ógnos*) loses accent; *logós* 'gatherer' keeps its suffix accent
- *sakrovéstrom* 'sacred place': *sakró-* loses accent; *véstrom* keeps its root accent
- *veikspotís* 'village master': *véiks-* loses accent; *potís* keeps its suffix accent

Note: This rule applies to determinative (tatpuruṣa) and possessive (bahuvrīhi) compounds. Dvandva and other types are not yet attested in the lexicon.
