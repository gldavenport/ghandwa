## Inscription Formulae

Four target formulae modelled on archaic IE epigraphic conventions (cf. Duenos inscription, Praeneste fibula).

### 1. Maker formula

> *[DEDICANT-nom] [RECIPIENT-dat] me tekseti.*

"[X] crafted me for [Y]."

- DEDICANT in nominative; examples:
  - *viros vezus* — "a good man" (nom.sg o-stem + o/ā adj)
- *me* — acc.sg 1sg pronoun (enclitic); the speaking object
- *tekseti* — 3sg thematic present of *tekseti ~ teksonti* "to weave, craft, fabricate"
- RECIPIENT in dative; examples:
  - *gvénāi* — "for the lady" (ā-stem dat.sg)
  - *divóimos* — "for the gods" (o-stem dat.pl of *deivós*)
- Word order: S–dat–O–V (recipient before object, object immediately preverbal)

Status: ✅ complete as template

**Worked example:**

> *viros vezus veneiei kārāi me tekseti.*
> wi.ˈros we.ˈzus we.ˈne.je͡j kaː.ˈraːj me ˈtek.se.ti
> "A good man crafted me for a dear beloved."

- *veneiei* — dat.sg of *venís* (i-stem mf) "lover, beloved"; i-stem dat.sg *-eiei*
- *kārāi* — dat.sg of *kārā* (fem. of *kāros*, o/ā adj) "dear"; ā-stem dat.sg *-āi*
- Two datives in apposition: epithet + noun, typical of archaic dedicatory register

### 2. Dedication formula

> *viros vezus me ðḗti divóimos.*

"A good man dedicates me to the gods."

- *ðḗti* — 3sg athematic present of *ðḗti ~ ðénti* "to put, place; to dedicate"
- *divóimos* — dat.pl of *deivós* "god" (o-stem dat.pl *-oimos*)
- Variant with human recipient:
  > *viros vezus me ðḗti gvénāi.*
  > "A good man dedicates me to the lady."

Status: ✅ complete as template

---

### 3. Apotropaic formula

> *ne me duzvirós ɣʷéneti.*

"Let no villain strike me."

- *ne* — prohibitive particle (sentential negation, lexicon #331)
- *duzvirós* — "ne'er-do-well, villain, evil-doer" (lexicon #334); compound *dus-* + *virós*
- *ɣʷéneti* — 3sg thematic present of *ɣʷéneti ~ ɣʷénonti* "to strike, beat, harm" (lexicon #429); used as jussive indicative (no subjunctive needed; parallel to Old Latin *-tōd* imperative in Duenos)

Notes:
- *dus-/duz-*: PIE pejorative prefix *\*dus-* (cf. Greek *δυσ-*, Sanskrit *dus-*); surface allomorph *duz-* before voiced sounds by regular s→z voicing rule

Status: ✅ complete — all entries canonical

---

### 4. Oath formula

> *óitom ðḗti divóimos.*

"He places an oath before the gods." (= "He swears before the gods.")

- *óitom* — acc.sg of *óitos* "oath, commitment" (lexicon #379); o-grade noun from PIE \*h₁ey- "to go"; shared by PC \*oitos and PGmc \*aiþaz — western isogloss
- *ðḗti* — 3sg athematic present of *ðḗti ~ ðénti* "to put, place; to dedicate" (lexicon #332); periphrastic oath construction parallels Hittite *lingain dai-* and Greek *hórkon tithénai*
- *divóimos* — dat.pl of *deivós* "god" (o-stem dat.pl *-oimos*)

Status: ✅ complete — all entries canonical; resolved as periphrastic collocation

## Extended Inscriptions & Oral-Register Texts

### 5. The Mountain Text (*montaβis eti kalniβis…*)

A didactic text in oral/chant register, structured around the instrumental case as a teaching device. Thematically connected to the ontological categories in the Lore file (things that grow / move / think / talk).

**English gloss (target):**

> People don't eat mountains and hills —  
> but by mountains and hills, people eat.  
> Listen and learn.  
> From mountains and hills, stones.  
> From stones, ores.  
> From ores, metals.  
> From metals, tools.  
> By tools, food.  
> By mountains and hills, food.  
> Everything is connected.

**Partially worked Ghandwa text (with gaps):**

```

[mountains-ins.pl.] eti [hills-ins.pl.] gamones edonti.
kved, klevee eti [see-imp.].
eks [mountains-abl.pl.] eti [hills-abl.pl.], [stones-acc.pl.].
eks [stones-abl.pl.], raudons.
eks raudois, [metals-acc.pl.].
[metals-ins.pl.], [tools-acc.pl.].
[tools-ins.pl.], [food-acc.pl.].
[mountains-ins.pl.] eti [hills-ins.pl.] gamones edonti.
```

Notes on partially-established forms:
- *gamones* — acc.pl of \**gamō ~ gamnes* 'person, human being' (cf. staging Round 1 consonant stem snippets: *(dʰ)ǵʰmṓ → ǥamō*); n-stem
- *edonti* — 3pl thematic present of *édeti ~ édonti* 'to eat' (lexicon #334) ✅
- *eti* — conjunction 'and'; cf. staging Round 4 #204; attested in notes as *kʷeti/-kʷe* > *pedi/-pe*; the note form *eti* may be a variant or earlier stage 🔲
- *raudons* — acc.pl of \**rouðos* 'ore, red metal' (cf. Round 4 #172 *rouðos ~ rouþas*; Ghandwa form pending)

## Notes on Verbal Register

- Jussive use of indicative present with *ne*: established for prohibitive inscriptions, parallel to archaic Italic *ne* + indicative
- Athematic verbs (*ðḗti*, *éiti*, *stā́ti*) carry sacred/formal register
- Thematic verbs (*tekseti*, *édeti*, *véideti*) are more neutral/everyday
