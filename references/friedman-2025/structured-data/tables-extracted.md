Modern Romanian has a slightly different form of this composite subordinator,
with ‘for’ (pentru) plus ca ‘as’ and the modal subordinator să; Aromanian differs
more significantly from this Balkan pattern, having simply ca să, and
Meglenoromanian has că si and other constructions (Atanasov 2002: 276).
