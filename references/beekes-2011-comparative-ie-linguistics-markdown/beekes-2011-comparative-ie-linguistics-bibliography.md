Bibliography

The bibliography is organized in three parts: I. General introduction, II. Language change, and III. Indo-European Linguistics. The focus is on English and German titles, less so on French. Of course, useful handbooks and specialist studies also appear in other languages, such as Czech, Danish, Dutch, Italian, Russian, Spanish, etc. The titles in Section 1 and 2 may be regarded as ‘further reading’, whereas in Section 3, the selection also refers to the basis on which the theories proposed in this book are founded.
