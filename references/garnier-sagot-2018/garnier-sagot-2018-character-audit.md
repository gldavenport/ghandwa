# Character-audit notes — Garnier & Sagot 2018

## Source-control checks performed

- Rendered PDF pages at 200 DPI and 400 DPI.
- Compared extracted text against page-image crops for the dense linguistic paragraph and selected references.
- Checked for residual extraction artifacts, including line-break hyphenation from the HAL cover page.
- Checked for characters likely to be damaged in PDF extraction: Greek μ vs micro sign µ, Greek beta symbol ϐ vs beta β, superscript ʷ, schwa ǝ, đ, macroned Greek υ in Λῡδός, Greek accents, and bibliography diacritics.

## Character decisions

- `µόλυβδος` from raw extraction was corrected to Greek `μόλυβδος`; the page image shows Greek mu, not an intended micro sign.
- `καλύϐη` and `κολοϐός` preserve the Greek beta-symbol `ϐ`, as shown by the PDF extraction and page image.
- `αὐτοκάβδαλος`, `καβδαλός`, `Κύρβαντες`, and `λυκάβαντ-` retain normal Greek beta `β`.
- `*morgwiyo-` from raw extraction was corrected to `*morgʷiyo-`; the page image shows superscript `w`.
- `/marǝwđa/` preserves `ǝ` and `đ`.
- `Λῡδός` preserves the macron on upsilon.
- `PAnat. *-VyV- > Lyd. -(V)dV-` preserves the source’s notation without expanding `y`.
- Bibliography entries preserve source spellings, including `Lydische Wörterbuch`, `Inschrifensammlung`, and `Amsterdam Atlanta: Rodopi`.

## Remaining limits

- The PDF is only two pages and born-digital; further passes are unlikely to change the main text substantially.
- Italic formatting has been encoded for visually meaningful title/bibliography cases and obvious technical/source-title uses. The extraction prioritizes character authority over exhaustive typographic mimicry.
