# Structured lexical comparanda

This table extracts the linguistic forms and reconstructed comparanda used in Kroonen 2016. It is designed for corpus use, not as an independent etymological judgment.

| ID | Language/stage | Form | Gloss/meaning | Role | Reconstruction/relation | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| L001 | Greek | `μῆλον` | apple; also crab apple, quince, apricot, citron, and other tree fruits | central Mediterranean comparandum | usually connected with *meh₂-lo-; Kroonen argues it can be explained from *smh₂l- via loss of initial *s before resonant and laryngeal vocalization | Attic/Ionic form; may be source for Latin/Albanian borrowing scenarios. |
| L002 | Doric Greek | `μᾶλον` | apple / fruit | dialectal Greek attestation | supports possible West Greek source for Latin mālum and Albanian mollë | Cited in discussion of first-millennium BC borrowing scenario. |
| L003 | Latin | `mālum` | apple; crab apple, quince, apricot, peach, citron, gourd | central Mediterranean comparandum | formally compatible with *meh₂-lo- but possibly borrowed from West Greek; also compatible with Kroonen's *smh₂l- account | Meaning range overlaps Greek μῆλον. |
| L004 | Albanian | `mollë` | apple | central Mediterranean/Balkan comparandum | compatible with *meh₂-lo- but possibly borrowed from West Greek or Latin | Orel 2000:31 cited for possible Latin borrowing. |
| L005 | Hittite | `šam(a)lu-` | apple | new Anatolian comparandum adduced by Kroonen | key evidence for revising *meh₂lo- to *smh₂l- | Attested with Sumerogram GIŠḪAŠḪUR ‘apple tree’. |
| L006 | Hittite | `maḫla-` | formerly ‘apple tree’; now generally ‘young grapevine’ | rejected older comparandum | older link to Greek/Latin apple words rejected on formal and semantic grounds | Cuny 1930 proposed the old comparison; Puhvel 1997 gives revised meaning. |
| L007 | Sumerogram | `GIŠḪAŠḪUR` | apple tree | logographic attestation context for Hittite šam(a)lu- | supports semantic identification of Hittite šam(a)lu- | Analyzed in the article as Sumerian geš + ḫašḫur. |
| L008 | Sumerian | `geš` | tree; wood | component of Sumerogram | part of GIŠḪAŠḪUR |  |
| L009 | Sumerian | `ḫašḫur` | apple | component of Sumerogram | part of GIŠḪAŠḪUR; compared with Akkadian ḫašḫūru- |  |
| L010 | Akkadian | `ḫašḫūru-` | apple | semantic comparator in explanation of Sumerogram | equivalent of Sumerian ḫašḫur |  |
| L011 | quasi-Indo-European / traditional reconstruction | `*meh₂-lo-` | apple / fruit etymon | traditional proto-form under criticism | Kroonen argues this should be corrected to *smh₂l- if Hittite šam(a)lu- is included | Limited distribution makes inherited Indo-European status unlikely. |
| L012 | proposed proto-form | `*smh₂l-` | apple / stone fruit? | Kroonen's improved proto-form | unifies Greek/Latin/Albanian and Hittite by regular sound changes | May represent borrowing rather than inherited PIE lexeme. |
| L013 | Greek-stage reconstruction | `*smh₂lo-` | apple / fruit | branch-specific form in borrowing scenario | o-stem outcome corresponding to Greek/central Mediterranean forms | Contrasted with Anatolian *smh₂lu-. |
| L014 | Anatolian-stage reconstruction | `*smh₂lu-` | apple | branch-specific form in borrowing scenario | u-stem outcome corresponding to Hittite šam(a)lu- | Suffixal variation may signal repeated borrowing. |
| L015 | Northwest European | `*h₂eb-ol-` | apple | alternative areal apple word; rejected as cognate of Hittite šam(a)lu- | previously connected with Hittite šam(a)lu- by Ivanov/Gamkrelidze/Ivanov/Zavaroni; Kroonen rejects connection | Would require ad hoc s-mobile and incorrect *-ml- > *-bl- change. |
| L016 | Proto-Kartvelian | `*msxal-` | pear | speculative deeper-contact comparandum | one metathesis short of *smh₂l- according to Kroonen | Cited from Klimov 1998:169; possible broad original meaning ‘stone fruit’. |
| L017 | PIE example | `*sm-ih₂ (*gʰes-l-ih₂)` | one / thousand syntagm example | sound-law illustration | shows initial *s before resonant retained/lost differently across branches | Compared with Greek μία and Latin mille. |
| L018 | Greek | `μία` | one | sound-law illustration | from PIE *sm-ih₂ in article's example |  |
| L019 | Latin | `mille` | one thousand | sound-law illustration | used in comparison with PIE *sm-ih₂ / *gʰes-l-ih₂ |  |
| L020 | Sanskrit | `sahásra-` | 1000 | footnote evidence for Indo-European age of syntagm | cited with Avestan hazaŋra- < PIE *sṃ-gʰéslo- |  |
| L021 | Avestan | `hazaŋra-` | 1000 | footnote evidence for Indo-European age of syntagm | cited with Sanskrit sahásra- < PIE *sṃ-gʰéslo- |  |
| L022 | PIE example | `*sṃ-gʰéslo-` | 1000 | footnote reconstruction | source of Sanskrit sahásra- and Avestan hazaŋra- in article's example |  |
| L023 | PIE example | `*tl̥h₂-tó-` | borne | sound-law illustration | illustrates *CRHC- > Greek/Latin CReHC- with lengthening after laryngeal loss | Rendered in article as PIE *tḷh₂-tó-; normalized here as l + ring below. |
