# Notes — Thorsø, Wigman et al. 2023 (Word Mining)

## Bibliographic details

**Rasmus Thorsø, Andrew Wigman, Anthony Jakob, Axel Palmér, Paulus van Sluis, Guus Kroonen et al. "Word mining: metal names and the Indo-European dispersal."**  
In: Kristiansen & Willerslev (eds.), *The Indo-European Puzzle Revisited*, pp. 105–126. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press, 2023.  
DOI: 10.1017/9781009261753.012

## Content summary

Uses metal terminology (copper, bronze, iron, gold, silver) across IE branches to reconstruct the timing and geography of the IE dispersal. Applies computational "word mining" methods to track which metal names are inherited vs. borrowed vs. independently innovated. Same volume as van Sluis et al. 2023 (Ch. 13) and Koch 2024 (Ch. 10).

## Ghandwa relevance

Metal vocabulary is a priority area for lexicon development — terms for copper, bronze, gold, and iron need careful treatment to distinguish PIE inheritance from NWIE-stage loans. This paper directly informs those decisions. Cross-reference with Koch 2020 and van Sluis 2023 on the shared Celto-Germanic vocabulary question.
