## Scope

This pass used the second-pass Markdown as the base and checked the second-pass unresolved flag list against rendered PDF page images. No OCR was used.

The pass concentrated on the dense form-heavy passages previously flagged as not quote-ready: pages 39-43, 46-47, 49, 73, 75-77, 80, 82, 89-90, 97, 102, 112-113, 120, 122-124, 134, and 137-138.

## Repairs made

- Restored many missing semivowels, especially `u̯`, in water/fire/sun/well-source reconstructions.
- Repaired the main Hittite water and fire forms where the PDF text layer dropped `u̯`, `ḫ`, and reduced-vowel notation.
- Repaired the most important Vedic/Sanskrit forms in the water and fire sections, including `udnáḥ`, `udā́`, `pāvakaḥ`, `pavaká-`, `pāvana-`, and related proto-forms.
- Repaired core heteroclite paradigms and derivational chains for water, fire, sun, and well/source where the second-pass text had blanks or deformed roots.
- Repaired selected udder, liver, excrement, springtime, and Tocharian fire forms in the flagged passages.
- Preserved page anchors and the overall Markdown structure.

## Remaining limitation

This is a stronger third-pass working edition, but it is still not a full diplomatic edition of the entire thesis. The original PDF text layer has systematic font-encoding damage in specialized linguistic forms. I checked and repaired the known second-pass problem clusters, but I did not manually verify every isolated form in all 158 pages. For formal quotation of a specific form outside the checked clusters, verify that individual form against the PDF image.
