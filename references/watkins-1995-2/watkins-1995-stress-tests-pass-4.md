## Evaluation

- The exact pass-3 marker `[unclear: non-Latin form omitted in OCR]` was eliminated from the main file. Remaining `[unclear: ...]` markers are now more specific and mostly limited to forms that could not be resolved safely.
- Greek/non-Latin lookup improved for high-salience examples: Apollo/Appaliunas, Homeric clitic `ἄρ(α)`, Heraclitus/Asius `βόρβορος`, Greek `κτεν-`, `ἀμφιέπω`, Dionysiac `θύσθλα`, `Θάνατος`, `Οὖτις`, `ῥόπαλον`, `μίτρη`, and Protesilaos `πρῶτος` / `λαοῦ`.
- The Index of Words is now arranged by page with preserved OCR lines and page anchors instead of the badly interleaved pass-3 tail. It is more useful for search, but entry-level proofreading is still needed for exact accents, Old Norse characters, Greek, and some language labels.
- Stress tests still show weak spots in Greek poetic quotations, metrical notation, and entries that require exact accentuation or special characters.

## Benefit assessment for suggested next passes

1. Bibliography normalization pass: moderate benefit. It would improve citation retrieval and downstream bibliographic use, but it will not materially improve the core formula/poetics content. Best done after the text/index/formula work unless bibliography search is the immediate need.
2. Formula/form-list extraction: high benefit. A companion TSV/CSV/Markdown dataset of formulas, roots, language forms, glosses, chapters, and page references would make the source much more useful for comparative searches and conlang/worldbuilding reuse.
3. Targeted chapter audit, especially chapters 27–59: high benefit. These chapters contain the densest dragon-slaying formulas, reconstructed roots, and cross-linguistic comparisons. A focused audit there would likely improve the most important material more than a whole-book generic pass.

Recommendation: the next most useful work is a combined formula/form-list extraction plus targeted audit of chapters 27–59. Bibliography normalization is worthwhile, but secondary unless citation cleanup is the immediate goal.
