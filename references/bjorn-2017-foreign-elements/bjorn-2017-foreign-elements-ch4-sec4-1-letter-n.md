## *N*

### 96. *néh₂-u-

Gloss: ‘boat’

Attestations: ⁽?⁾Toch. A new ‘flood’; Lat. nāvis; OIr. nāu; ON nór ‘ship’; Gr. ναῦς; Ved. nāu-

Notes: It has been suggested that the noun is derived from the verbal root *(s)neh₂- ‘to swim’ (LIV²: 572f.) with convincing cognates from Anatolian, too, which seems semantically cogent, but curiously lacks reflexes of the initial *s- of the root. Amble evidence thus suggests an internal derivation after the breakup of Anatolian, and likely also Tocharian.

External comparanda: Semitic: *’unw-(at-) > Ugaritic ‘anyt ‘ship’, Arab. ‘inā’- ‘vessel’

Discussion: Gamkrelidze & Ivanov suggest that the term was borrowed into PIE from Semitic (1995: 771f.), but, due to the proposed internal PIE derivation, Mallory & Adams’ rejection of this comparison (1997: 74) seems well-founded (cf. also Diakonoff 1985: 125).
