Biographies

**Jenny Helena Larsson** is Professor of Baltic Languages and Director of the Centre for Indo-European Language and Culture at Stockholm University. She is co-PI of the National Centre of Excellence _Centre for the Human Past_ and PI of the research programme _Languages and Myths in Prehistory_ (LAMP).
ORCID: <https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7251-3513>
**Anders Richardt Jørgensen** obtained his PhD in Indo-European linguistics from the University of Copenhagen in 2007. His research focuses on comparative Indo-European and Celtic linguistics, Middle Breton philology and Modern Breton dialectology. He is currently a researcher at Uppsala University in the research programme _Languages and Myths in Prehistory_ (LAMP).
ORCID: <https://orcid.org/0009-0003-5317-9227>
**Thomas Olander** is Professor of Indo-European Studies at the _Roots of Europe_ centre at the University of Copenhagen. His research interests include linguistic reconstruction and the relationship between reconstructed languages and the archaeological record. He is PI of the research project _The Indo-European Dark Ages Illuminated_ and a member of the research programme _Languages and Myths in Prehistory_ (LAMP).
ORCID: <https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7938-2827>
**Davide Ermacora** holds a doctoral degree in Anthropology from a joint program between the University of Turin (Italy) and Lumière University Lyon 2 (France). He is the author of numerous publications in the fields of religious history, supernatural beliefs, and folkloric traditions across various time periods. He is currently completing a book on the midwife-witch stereotype.
ORCID: <https://orcid.org/0000-0002-6908-4500>
**Riccardo Ginevra** is _Ricercatore_

<!-- p. 244 -->

in Historical and General Linguistics at the Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore in Milan and affiliated with the research programme _Language and Myths of Prehistory_ (LAMP). He has previously worked as postdoc at the University of Copenhagen’s research centre _Roots of Europe_ and at Harvard University’s _Center for Hellenic Studies_. His research mainly deals with the historical and comparative linguistics of ancient and medieval Indo-European languages, with a special focus on topics of etymology, phraseology and comparative mythology.
ORCID: <https://orcid.org/0000-0002-6731-6494>
**Anders Kaliff** is Professor of Archaeology at Uppsala University. Before taking up his position in Uppsala, he had a long career at the Swedish National Heritage Board, as a field archaeologist and as head of the Department of Archaeological Excavations. He is a member of the research programme _Language and Myths of Prehistory_ (LAMP), a working member of the _Royal Gustavus Adolfus Academy_ and the chairman of the board of the _Center for Indo-European Language and Culture_ at Stockholm University.
ORCID: <https://orcid.org/0000-0002-0611-2631>
**Terje Oestigaard** works at Linnaeus University, Kalmar, Sweden. He obtained his doctoral degree from the University of Bergen in 2004. He has conducted fieldwork in several countries in Africa and Asia and has a particular focus on death rituals and water studies. He is also researcher at Uppsala University and a member of the research programme _Languages and Myths in Prehistory_ (LAMP).
ORCID: <https://orcid.org/0000-0001-7775-5434>
**Birgit Anette Olsen** is Professor of Indo-European Studies at the _Roots of Europe_ centre at the University of Copenhagen, and a member of the research programme _Language and Myths of Prehistory_ (LAMP). She has a broad interest in Indo-European language and culture, and her research includes works on nominal morphology and morphonology, the historical background of Classical Armenian and archaeolinguistics, particularly the structure of early Indo-European societies.
ORCID: <https://orcid.org/0000-0002-8064-7351>
**Peter Jackson Rova** is

<!-- p. 245 -->

Professor in the History of Religions at Stockholm University and affiliated with the research programme _Language and Myths of Prehistory_ (LAMP).
He specializes in Germanic, Greek and Indo-Iranian religions with an additional broad interest in the theory, method and conceptual underpinnings of religious studies. Among his recent publications are the co-edited volume _Philosophy and the end of sacrifice: Disengaging ritual in Ancient India, Greece and beyond_, the monograph _Devotion and artifice: Themes of suspension in the history of religions_ and the forthcoming monograph _Indogermanische Religion: Rekonstruktion der Götterwelt, Mythologie und Kultsprache_, co-written with Norbert Oettinger.
ORCID: <https://orcid.org/0000-0002-0742-6640>
