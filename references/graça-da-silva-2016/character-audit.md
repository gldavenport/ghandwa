# Character audit

This pass checked high-risk characters and extraction artifacts in the article.

## Changes made

- Corrected the PIE metal-word notation in the discussion from `*aios*` to `*ai⌢os*`, preserving the source’s inserted tie/frown glyph (`U+2322`) between `i` and `o`.
- Rechecked Greek/statistical symbols used in the methods and figures: `λ`, `θ`, `β`, `α`, and inequality phrasing were preserved in clean Unicode text.
- Rechecked author and reference-name diacritics visible in the article and bibliography, including `Graça`, `d’Huy`, `Swahn Jö`, and `Praehistorische Zeitschrift`.
- Rechecked Figure 4 tale-type labels with suffixes and diacritics, including `314A*`, `425C`, `425E`, `425M`, `460B`, `471A`, `510B`, `650A`, `735A`, `Peau d’Âne`, and `Peau d’Asne`.

## Source spellings retained

- Reference 14 reads `Selected papers on folkore` in the PDF extraction and page image. It has been retained as source text, with a silent Markdown comment noting that it appears to be a source typo rather than correcting it to `folklore`.
- Reference 17 reads `State of New York University Press` in the source and is retained as printed.

## Remaining limits

- This is a character audit of the article text and Figure 4, not an independent verification against the article’s electronic supplementary material.
- No full external bibliographic authority reconciliation was performed.
