The Roman army is known to have used glandes missiles at least since the end of the 3rd C. BC, and they fell into disuse by the beginnings of the Christian Era. The inscriptions borne by these bullets are usually much simpler than in our case, however, and can be classified according to the following typology: commands and exclamations, names of places or origonyms, personal names, and, crucially, names of divinities whose assistance was invoked in battle.
