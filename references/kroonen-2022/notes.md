# Notes — Kroonen et al. 2022

## Bibliographic details

**Guus Kroonen, Anthony Jakob, Axel I. Palmér, Paulus van Sluis & Andrew Wigman. "Indo-European cereal terminology suggests a Northwest Pontic homeland for the core Indo-European languages."**  
*PLOS ONE* (2022).  
Institutions: Leiden University Centre for Linguistics; University of Copenhagen.

## Content summary

Uses cereal and agricultural terminology across IE branches to argue for a Northwest Pontic homeland for core (non-Anatolian) Indo-European. Lexical evidence for grain vocabulary is analyzed for distribution patterns, reconstruction depth, and substrate vs. inherited status. Part of the archaeogenetics-adjacent literature integrating linguistic and archaeological/genetic evidence.
