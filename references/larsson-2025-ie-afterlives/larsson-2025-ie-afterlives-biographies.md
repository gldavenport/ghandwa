# Biographies

Jenny H. Larsson is Professor of Baltic Languages and Director of the Centre for Indo-European Language and Culture at Stockholm University. She is co-PI of the National Centre of Excellence Centre for the Human Past and PI of the research programme Languages and Myths in Prehistory (LAMP).

ORCID: [https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7251-3513](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7251-3513)

Thomas Olander is Professor of Indo-European Studies at the Roots of Europe centre at the University of Copenhagen. His research interests include linguistic reconstruction and the relationship between reconstructed languages and the archaeological record. He is PI of the research project The Indo-European Dark Ages Illuminated and a member of the research programme Languages and Myths in Prehistory (LAMP).

ORCID: [https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7938-2827](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-7938-2827)

Anders Richardt Jørgensen obtained his PhD in Indo-European linguistics from the University of Copenhagen in 2007. His research focuses on comparative Indo-European and Celtic linguistics, Middle Breton philology and Modern Breton dialectology. He is currently a researcher at Uppsala University in the research programme Languages and Myths in Prehistory (LAMP).

ORCID: [https://orcid.org/0009-0003-5317-9227](https://orcid.org/0009-0003-5317-9227)

Peter Jackson Rova is Professor in the History of Religions at Stockholm University and affiliated with the research programme Language and Myths of Prehistory (LAMP). He specializes in Germanic, Greek and Indo-Iranian religions with an additional broad interest in the theory, method and conceptual underpinnings of religious studies. Among his recent publications are the co-edited volume Philosophy and the end of sacrifice: Disengaging ritual in Ancient India, Greece and beyond, the monograph Devotion and artifice: Themes of suspension in the

ORCID: [https://orcid.org/0000-0002-0742-6640](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-0742-6640)

Martin Schwartz is Professor Emeritus of Iranian Studies at the Department of Middle Eastern Languages and Cultures, University of California, Berkeley. He received his PhD in Iranian studies at UC Berkeley in under the direction of Walter Brunio Henning in 1967/1968. After three years of teaching Ancient Iranian and Sanskrit at Columbia University, he taught for forty years at UC Berkeley. His copious publications address many aspects of Old, Middle and New Iranian linguistics and culture, as well as Indo-European Studies. He has in recent years been more and more focused on the textual-linguistic, structural- stylistic poetics and religious aspects of Zarathushtra’s Gathas, to which study he has introduced many innovative discoveries.

ORCID: [https://orcid.org/0009-0001-9929-4410](https://orcid.org/0009-0001-9929-4410)

Laura Massetti is Researcher at University of Naples “L’Orientale”, where she teaches Greek Language and Literature. Her main research interests include Greek Literature, Historical Linguistics, Indo-Iranian Languages, Comparative Religion, Mythology and Poetics. She is PI of the project The Lords of the Rings: A Comparative Lens on Ring-Compositions of Greek Lyric Poetry (LORACOLA).

ORCID: [https://orcid.org/0000-0002-5435-853X](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-5435-853X)

Jan N. Bremmer is Emeritus Professor of Religious Studies at the University of Groningen, Netherlands. His research focuses on Greek and Roman religion, Early Christianity and their reception in modern times. His most recent book is Jews, Pagans and Christians in the Roman Empire (2025).

ORCID: [https://orcid.org/0000-0001-8400-7143](https://orcid.org/0000-0001-8400-7143)

Michael Janda is Professor of Indo-European Linguistics at the University of Münster. His research focuses on Homer, the language and culture of ancient Greece and the Indo-Europeans, and in particular the origin and development of prehistoric and ancient myths.

Riccardo Ginevra is Assistant Professor of Historical and General Linguistics at the Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore in Milan and affiliated with the research programme Language and Myths of Prehistory (LAMP). He has previously worked as postdoc at the University of Copenhagen’s research centre Roots of Europe and at Harvard University’s Center for Hellenic Studies. His research mainly deals with the historical and comparative linguistics of ancient and medieval Indo-European languages, with a special focus on topics of etymology, phraseology and comparative mythology.

ORCID: [https://orcid.org/0000-0002-6731-6494](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-6731-6494)

Birgit Anette Olsen is Professor of Indo-European Studies at the Roots of Europe centre at the University of Copenhagen, and a member of the research programme Language and Myths of Prehistory (LAMP). She has a broad interest in Indo-European language and culture, and her research includes works on nominal morphology and morphonology, the historical background of Classical Armenian and archaeolinguistics, particularly the structure of early Indo-European societies.

ORCID: [https://orcid.org/0000-0002-8064-7351](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-8064-7351)

Anders Kaliff is Professor of Archaeology at Uppsala University. Before taking up his position in Uppsala, he had a long career at the Swedish National Heritage Board, as a field archaeologist and as head of the Department of Archaeological Excavations. He is a member of the research programme Language and Myths of Prehistory (LAMP), a working member of the Royal Gustavus Adolfus Academy and the chairman of the board of the Center for Indo-European Language and Culture at Stockholm University.

ORCID: [https://orcid.org/0000-0002-0611-2631](https://orcid.org/0000-0002-0611-2631)

Terje Oestigaard works at Linnaeus University, Kalmar, Sweden. He obtained his doctoral degree from the University of Bergen in 2004. He has conducted fieldwork in several countries in Africa and Asia and has a particular focus on death rituals and water studies. He is also researcher at Uppsala University and a member of the research programme Languages and Myths in Prehistory (LAMP).

ORCID: [https://orcid.org/0000-0001-7775-5434](https://orcid.org/0000-0001-7775-5434)
