## Extraction method

- Used the born-digital PDF text layer rather than OCR.
- Converted private-use numeral glyphs to ordinary Arabic numerals.
- Expanded common ligatures such as `ﬁ` and `ﬂ`.
- Reflowed paragraphs and repaired obvious line-break hyphenation.
- Inserted page anchors in the same style used for Chapters 2-4.
- Omitted repeated page headers and the publication footer from the body text.

## Notation issues

Chapter 1 has little dense linguistic notation. The only item that may warrant later stage-sensitive normalization is the reference to PIE `*gwh` in section 1.1. It is left in Ringe's extracted inline style rather than converted to `*gʷʰ`, because it occurs in an introductory reference to a later Germanic-transition discussion and should be checked only if a fully diplomatic edition is required.

## Quality assessment

This chapter is low-risk compared with Chapters 2-4. It contains no complex paradigms, no large tables, and no figure requiring image replacement. A second pass is probably unnecessary unless the whole-book package later applies a stricter house style to hyphens, dashes, roman numerals, or PIE inline notation.
