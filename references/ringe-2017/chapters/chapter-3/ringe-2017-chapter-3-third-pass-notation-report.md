## Purpose
The second pass over-normalized labiovelar notation by converting too many Germanic-stage `kw/gw/hw` sequences into superscript `kʷ/gʷ/hʷ`. This pass corrects explicit pre-PGmc, PGmc, and later Germanic-stage forms while preserving PIE and unlabeled transitional forms for later verification.

## Policy
- PIE and Proto-Core/Proto-Central IE forms generally remain in Unicode notation: `*kʷ`, `*gʷ`, `*gʷʰ`.
- Explicit `pre-PGmc`, `PGmc`, `PNWGmc`, `PWGmc`, `NWGmc`, `WGmc`, `Gmc`, and attested Germanic-stage contexts use plain `kw/gw/hw`.
- Unlabeled transitional forms are not globally converted; they should be checked manually when cited.
- Dense tables and paradigms remain corpus-usable but not a diplomatic transcription.
