# Structured lexical dataset

This dataset separates Kroonen’s six primary Germanic root-noun candidates from additional comparanda used in the argument. It is not an independent etymological judgment; it records the article’s claims in a structured form.

## root-noun-001: PGm. *arwīt- ‘pea’

- **Category:** primary Germanic root noun
- **Section:** 3
- **Article Status:** demonstrably non-Indo-European; root noun
- **Germanic Evidence:** OHG arawīz/araweiz; MHG ar(e)weiz/arwīz/erbiz; OS erit; MLG erwete; MDu. a(e)rwete/arweete; Du. ert; ON plural ertr; OSw. ært, pl. ærter
- **Non Germanic Comparanda:** Gr. ἐρέβινϑος, ὄροβος; Lat. ervum; perhaps OIr. orbaind; Georgian/OCS/Mozarabic forms treated as Greek loans in footnote 5
- **Substrate Marker Or Morphology:** Root-noun inflection; Pre-Greek/European agricultural substrate suffix *-ĩd- / *-ind(h)-; comparison with Greek -ινϑος
- **Proposed Source Or Layer:** Agricultural substrate / Near Eastern crop Wanderwort mediated into Europe
- **Notes:** Central supporting example for unity of Germanic and Pre-Greek agricultural substrate.

## root-noun-002: PGm. *gait- ‘goat’

- **Category:** primary Germanic root noun
- **Section:** 4
- **Article Status:** root noun; argued non-Indo-European/post-PIE Wanderwort
- **Germanic Evidence:** ON geit, pl. geitr; OE gāt; OHG geiz
- **Non Germanic Comparanda:** Lat. haedus; discussion of regional goat words including *dig-, *bhuǵ-, *h₂eiǵ-, *h₂eǵ-, *kapr-, Semitic *gadi̯-, Caucasian comparanda
- **Substrate Marker Or Morphology:** Root-noun inflection; semantics tied to goat-keeping/agricultural-pastoral culture; limited areal distribution
- **Proposed Source Or Layer:** Old continuum of agricultural and cattle-breeding cultures expanding into Europe; not direct Proto-Semitic in Kroonen’s preferred interpretation
- **Notes:** Used against a deeply agricultural Proto-Indo-European model.

## root-noun-003: PGm. *hnit- ‘nit’

- **Category:** primary Germanic root noun
- **Section:** 5
- **Article Status:** root noun with unclear IE etymology; substrate origin possible
- **Germanic Evidence:** ON gnit, pl. gnitr; OE hnitu; OHG niz
- **Non Germanic Comparanda:** Ru. gnída; Latv. gnĩda; Gr. κονίς, -ίδος; Alb. thëní; Arm. anic; MIr. sned; Lith. glìnda; Lat. lēns, lendis
- **Substrate Marker Or Morphology:** Root-noun inflection; irregular European correspondences; *-id / *-ind alternation linked to agricultural substrate morphology
- **Proposed Source Or Layer:** Possible European substrate form approximated as *c~ʕ(o)n-ĩd
- **Notes:** Less culturally diagnostic than pea/goat, but formally relevant because of *-ind.

## root-noun-004: PGm. *hnut- ‘nut’

- **Category:** primary Germanic root noun
- **Section:** 6
- **Article Status:** root noun; non-Indo-European origin assumed
- **Germanic Evidence:** ON hnot, pl. hnøtr/hnetr; OE hnutu, pl. hnyte; OHG nuz, pl. nuzzi
- **Non Germanic Comparanda:** Lat. nux, gen. -cis; OIr. cnú, gen. cnó; W cneuen, pl. cnau
- **Substrate Marker Or Morphology:** Root-noun inflection; incompatible West Indo-European codas; possible final glottal stop or *-ũd(h)- substrate element
- **Proposed Source Or Layer:** West European non-Indo-European substrate/contact word
- **Notes:** Kroonen treats Celtic-Germanic similarity as suspect absent a Germano-Celtic proto-language.

## root-noun-005: PGm. *edis- / *idis- ~ *dīsi- ‘lady, woman of high standing; goddess/matron’

- **Category:** primary Germanic root noun
- **Section:** 7
- **Article Status:** root noun with potentially non-Indo-European origin
- **Germanic Evidence:** OE idis; OHG itis; OS idis; ON dís; cultural parallel dís Skjǫldunga / idis Scildinga
- **Non Germanic Comparanda:** No secure extra-Germanic cognates accepted; Indo-European etymologies via *h₁edh-ḗs or *h₂eidh- are rejected as semantically/formally weak
- **Substrate Marker Or Morphology:** Root-noun inflection; internal Germanic *edis ~ *dīsi- alternation compared speculatively with a-prefixation and stress-related reduction
- **Proposed Source Or Layer:** Local prehistoric North-European substrate
- **Notes:** Important because linguistic and mythological evidence are used together.

## root-noun-006: PGm. *wisund- ‘bison’

- **Category:** primary Germanic root/consonant noun
- **Section:** 8
- **Article Status:** probably originally consonant/root noun; non-Indo-European migratory term
- **Germanic Evidence:** OHG wisunt/-ant; ON visundr; OE we(o)send; OHG plural wisunti; proper name Wirunt; Gothic Οὐισανδος in Procopius
- **Non Germanic Comparanda:** OPruss. wissambs’; Lith. stum̃bras; Latv. sumbrs/sūbrs; OCS zǫbrь; Ru. iz’ubr’; Caucasian comparanda including Ossetic/Adygh/Georgian dombaj and Abkhaz a-dəwp-èy
- **Substrate Marker Or Morphology:** Consonant/root-noun behavior; irregular Balto-Slavic/Germanic forms; possible *wi- prefix; non-IE coda variation *-ombr / *-ond
- **Proposed Source Or Layer:** Extinct North-European language / migratory term entering Germanic and Balto-Slavic separately
- **Notes:** Kroonen dates adoption before Proto-Germanic breakup but after Proto-Balto-Slavic split, roughly mid-first millennium BC.

## comparandum-001: PGm. *sturjan-, -jōn- / BSl. *asetr- or *esetr- ‘sturgeon’

- **Category:** substrate-marker comparandum
- **Section:** 1
- **Article Status:** possible a-prefixation candidate; likely non-Indo-European
- **Germanic Evidence:** ON styrja; OHG sturio; OE styrgia
- **Non Germanic Comparanda:** Ru. osëtr; SCr. jèsetra; Lith. erškẽtras; OPruss. esketres
- **Substrate Marker Or Morphology:** Alternation of initial vowel in Balto-Slavic vs zero in Germanic; discussed under a-prefixation
- **Proposed Source Or Layer:** European substrate/contact word
- **Notes:** Presented as candidate despite Schrijver’s reduction rule not applying straightforwardly.

## comparandum-002: Greek ἄγλις ~ γέλγις ‘garlic head’

- **Category:** substrate-marker comparandum
- **Section:** 3
- **Article Status:** Pre-Greek substrate key case
- **Germanic Evidence:** None direct; used to interpret PGm. *arwīt- suffix morphology
- **Non Germanic Comparanda:** Greek γέλγις, gen. -ῑδος/-ῑϑος; ἄγλις, gen. -ῑϑος
- **Substrate Marker Or Morphology:** Combines a-prefixation with *-ĩd(h)- / -ινϑ- type suffix; root alternation *a-ggl-ĩd(h)- / *gegl-ĩd(h)-
- **Proposed Source Or Layer:** Pre-Greek / European agricultural substrate
- **Notes:** Used to argue that a-prefixation and the ind(h)-suffix belong to the same substrate complex.

## comparandum-003: PGm. *þeura- / *steura- ‘bull, steer’

- **Category:** substrate/contact comparandum
- **Section:** 7
- **Article Status:** borrowed/contact word, not inherited PIE in Kroonen’s treatment
- **Germanic Evidence:** ON þjórr; Dutch dialect duur; Gothic/OE/OHG steer forms under *steura-
- **Non Germanic Comparanda:** Lat. taurus; Gr. tauros; Lith. taũras; OCS tur; Alb. ter; OIr. tarb; Proto-Semitic *ṭawr; Etr. thevrumines
- **Substrate Marker Or Morphology:** Irregular *t/*st/*þ correspondences; northern *e vs Latin *a; possible fronting of *a in source/intermediary language
- **Proposed Source Or Layer:** Semitic or intermediary Wanderwort/contact layer
- **Notes:** Used as possible support for fronted *a > *e in pre-Germanic source material.

## comparandum-004: PIE *h₂ster- / PSem. *ᶜaṭtar- ‘star; Venus/star goddess’

- **Category:** substrate/contact comparandum
- **Section:** 7
- **Article Status:** possible external Wanderwort/contact item discussed as analogue
- **Germanic Evidence:** Go. stairno among IE comparanda
- **Non Germanic Comparanda:** Hitt. ḫasterza; Gr. ἀστήρ; Lat. stella; Skt. tāraḥ / stŕ̥bhiḥ; Akk. ištar; Hebr. ᶜaštōreṭ; SArab. ᶜṭtr
- **Substrate Marker Or Morphology:** Irregular Sanskrit forms; possible substitution of Semitic ayin and *ṭt cluster by h₂ and *(s)t
- **Proposed Source Or Layer:** Proto-Semitic or external source entering IE dialect continuum
- **Notes:** Not a Germanic root noun; included because it supports Kroonen’s broader argument about Wanderwort phonological irregularity.

## comparandum-005: Lat. aper / PGm. *epr / BSl. *wepr ‘wild boar’

- **Category:** substrate-marker comparandum
- **Section:** 8
- **Article Status:** non-Indo-European word with possible *wi-/w alternation
- **Germanic Evidence:** OE eofor; OHG ebur
- **Non Germanic Comparanda:** Lat. aper; OCS veprь; Latv. vepris
- **Substrate Marker Or Morphology:** Initial *w vs zero possibly compared with *dzombr ~ *widzombr in bison word
- **Proposed Source Or Layer:** European substrate/contact word
- **Notes:** Included as comparandum for the proposed *wi- prefix in *wisund-.
