# Etymological Dictionary of the Baltic Inherited Lexicon

## Dictionary — Latvian — B

<!-- source-pdf-page: 541; source-page: 527 -->
### bariba
**LATV** bariba 'food'
**BSL** *bar-($)-
**PSL** *boršbno n. o (e) 'flour, food'
**SL** OCS brašbno 'food'; Ru. b6rošno (dial.) 'rye-flour'; ORu. borošbno
          '(farinaceous) food'; Ukr. b6rošno 'flour'; SCr. brašno 'flour, food'; Sln.
          brašnų 'food'; Bulg. brašn6 'flour'
**PIE** *bhar-(e )s-
**IE** Lat. far n. 'spelt, flour'; Lat. farina f. 'flour'; Got. barizeins 'barley-'; Olc. barr
          m. 'grain'; Olr. bairgen f. 'bread, loaf', W bara 'bread'
We are probably dealing here with a noun *bhar-, which was borrowed into IndoEuropean (cf. Schrijver 1991: 113-114).

<!-- source-pdf-page: 542; source-page: 528 -->
### brauna
**LATV** braūna [aū, au 2] 'flake, scale, abandoned skin or shell, caul, entrails'; VAR
          braūi;za
**PIE** *bhrouH-n- (*bhrou-Hn-?)
**IE** Skt. bhrū1;1a- m. 'embryo'; Oir. bru f. 'abdomen, belly, bowels, interior'
On semantic grounds I have separated this etymon from →briauna 'edge'. Unlike
Fraenkel, I am hesitant about the connection with →briauti(s ).
