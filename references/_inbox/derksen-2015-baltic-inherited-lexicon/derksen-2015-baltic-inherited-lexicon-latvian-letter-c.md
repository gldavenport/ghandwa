# Etymological Dictionary of the Baltic Inherited Lexicon

## Dictionary — Latvian — C

<!-- source-pdf-page: 542; source-page: 528 -->
### cenksle
**LATV** ceiiksle [eii, en2] 'hamstring, (pl.) feet, leg, (en 2) 'hollow of the knee'; VAR
         cenkste (Ulm.) 'hamstring'
See →kinka. We also find e-grade of the root in →kenkll.

<!-- source-pdf-page: 542; source-page: 528 -->
### cerme
**LATV** c[unclear]rme 'intestinal worm'; VAR cermis 2; cęrms 2
See →kirmis.

<!-- source-pdf-page: 542; source-page: 528 -->
### cimslis
**LATV** cimslis 'tendon, hamstring, shank'; VAR cimsla 'tendon, hamstring, shank'
**IE** OE ham, hom f. 'back of the knee'; OHG hama, hamma f. 'shank, back of
          the knee'
As argued s.v. →kinka, Latv. cim- may be analyzed as *knH-m-.

### cinksla
**LATV** cinksla [ in] 'calf, (in) hamstring, der untere Teil <les Hinterfu.Bes'; VAR
          cifikslis
See →kinka.

<!-- source-pdf-page: 543; source-page: 529 -->
### cipsla
**LATV** cipsla [i, i2, 12] 'tendon'; VAR cipsla (Ulm„ Nigr.)
It is highly uncertain if this noun is somehow etymologically connected with → Latv.
cinksla. The root cfp- seems to be circumflex rather than acute and may not contain a
nasal element at all.
