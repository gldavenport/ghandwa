§6.4

THE WORD FOR ‘POLE, PROP’

The development of *eh₂ -' > *a - in * heh₂ m-' > * a m- finds a parallel in the development of ON staurr ‘pole’. The word has an exact cognate in Gr. σταυρός ‘pole’ < *steh₂ [unclear] s. For this attractive derivation to work in Germanic, Dybo’s Law has to affect the sequence *-eh₂u- before Mahlow’s Law could have affected it and eliminated its semi-vowel (I return to the relative chronology of sound changes in §7.2.4 on p.138). It is striking that the accent in Gr. σταυρός is on the suffix, which can then also account for the development of *-eh₂u-' > PG *-a - by Dybo’s Law, or: *steh₂ ró- > *stō rá > *sta [unclear] -. It is likely that ON stórr, OFri. stō , OS stō , OHG stuori, all: ‘great, strong, heavy’ < *stō - are cognate. Semantically, the connection is possible if the meaning of ‘pole, prop’ was used ethically, using the supporting strength of the pole metaphorically and developing from there.
