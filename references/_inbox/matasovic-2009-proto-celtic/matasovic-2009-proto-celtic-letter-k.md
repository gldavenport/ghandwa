# Dictionary: K

### *kwal-na- 'go around' [Vb]
 GOID: OIr. imm-cella 'surrounds'
 W: MW pallu 'die, perish, be destroyed, run out, fail'; palla [3s Pres.]
 PIE: *kwelh₁- 'turn' (IEW: 639f.)
 COGN: Skt. carati 'moves, goes', Lat. colo 'inhabit, take care of, cultivate'
 ETYM: OIr. imm-cella < *ambi-kwal-na- (the simplex verb is unattested in
Goidelic). The reconstruction of the meaning ofPCelt. *kwal-na- is tentative,
since the reflexes underwent significant semantic changes, and the simplex
verb is unattested. Celtic preserved the zero-grade of the PIE root
(*kwl-neh₁-, with analogical replacement of PIE *-neh₁- > PCelt. *-nI- with
the productive suffix *-na-).
 REF: KPV 427ff., LIV 386-388, LEIA C-61, GPC III: 2676.

### *kwantyo- 'flat hill' [Noun]
 GOID: MIr. ceite [io and iii, m and fJ 'hill, eminence, open space, assembly'
 W: MW pant 'valley'
 BRET: OBret. pantet gl. imminet, MBret. pant em]
 CO: Co. pans 'valley'
 PIE: *kwem-t- 'hill'
 COGN: Lat. cumulus 'hill', OE hwamm 'projection', Ole. hvammr 'small
angular valley'
 ETYM: The alternation between an io- and an iii-stem in MIr. probably
shows that this word is a substantivized adjective; the original meaning
could have been 'protruding' vel. sim. The reading and the meaning of
OBret. pantet are uncertain (another possible reading is Lat. pandit). The IE
etymology is very problematic. Lat. cumulus can also be derived from
*kumos, which is in turn from *kuh₂mo- 'heap' (cf. Gr. kYma 'wave,
sprout'), perhaps with *u > u by Dybo's law (de Vaan 2008: 152f.).
 REF: LEIA C-58, DGVB 280, Deshayes 2003: 556, de Bernardo Stempel
1999: 273.

### *kWaryo- 'cauldron' [Noun]
 GOID: OIr. coire [io m]
 W: MW peir [m] (GPC pair)
 BRET: MoBret. per [m]
 CO: OCo. per gl. lebes
 PIE: *kweru- 'cauldron' (IEW: 642)
 COGN: Skt. caro- 'cauldron, vase', Russ. cara, OE hwer
 ETYM: Propairol 'jar' is from the Gaulish reflex of this PCelt. word. The
yo-stem in Celtic must be secondary, since all other evidence points to a u-
stem, PIE *kweru-s; PCelt. *kwar_ could have been generalized from the
zero-grade *kwrw_, or the u-stem was replaced early by an i-stem *kweri-s /
*kWry-os, from the oblique cases of which we have PCelt. *kWaryo_. Russ.
tara, if related, must represent a Slavic Vfddhi formation (rather than the
inherited PIE lengthened grade of the root).
 REF: LEIA C-153, GPC III: 2669, EIEC 443, Deshayes 2003: 572,
Matasović 2004: 114.

### *kwaso- 'cough' [Noun]
 GOID: MIr. casachtach [a f] (DIL cosachtach) 'the act of coughing'
 W: W pas [m] 'whooping cough'
 BRET: OBret. pas gl. catarrus, MBret. pas, paz
 CO: Co. pas, MoCo. paz
 PIE: *kwehzs-t- 'cough' (IEW: 649)
 COGN: Skt. kds- 'cough', OHG huosto, Lith. kosti, Russ. kciselj
 ETYM: The Celtic forms are from the zero-grade *kwhzst- of this originally

### *kwati- 'chaff, husks' [Noun]
 GOID: OIr. caith [i f] gl. furfur

### *kwef- 'pant, breathe' [Vb]
 W: W peuo 'roar, breathe'
 PIE: *kwep- 'breathe, pant' (IEW: 596)
 COGN: Lith. kvepiit 'breathe'
 ETYM: This is not a very strong etymology, since the primary meaning of
W peuo, peuaf (according to GPC) is 'bellow, roar', and only secondarily
'pant, breathe'. The Welsh verb is attested late, only in the 16th century.
Moreover, other putative IE cognates in IEW (apart from Lith. kvepiit and
the related Baltic forms) can be easily dismissed.
 REF: GPC III: 2790, Fowkes 1945, LIV 336.

### *kwenno-'head' [Noun]
 GOID: OIr. cenn [0 n > m], Ogam CUNA-CENNI

Falileyev 129, Campanile 1974: 86, Deshayes 2003: 567, Ziegler 1994: 111,

### *kwerxt- 'bush' (?) [Noun]
 GOID: MIr. ceirt 'apple-tree, letter q in Ogam'
 W: MW perth 'bush, hedge, thicket' [f]
 CO: Co. Pen-berth [Toponym]
 PIE: *perkwu- 'oak' (IEW: 822)
 COGN: Lat. quercus, OEfurh 'fir'
 ETYM: Since MIr. ceirt is known only from glossaries and as the name of
the Ogam letter Q, this etymology is not very strong. Even so, at least the
derivation of W perth from PIE *perkW-t- may be regular: first *p ...kw
assimilated to *kw...kw, then *kw > *x before *t, and finally initial *kw > p in
Brittonic. The ancient name of the Ardennes, Hercynia (silva), Herkynios
(drymos) is usually derived from PIE *perkw-unyo- > PCelt. *ferkunyo-,
Gaul. *erkunyo- (cp. Go. fairguni 'mountain'). If this etymology is correct,
it shows that the regular assimilation of *pw...*kw to *k ...*kw occurred after
the delabialization of *kw before *u.
 REF: LEIA C-56, GPC III: 2780, Lambert 1994: 37.

### *kwes 'who' [Pron]
 GOID: OIr. cia
 W: OW pui, MW pwy
 BRET: OBret. pou, MBret. piou
 CO:Co.pyw
 PIE: *kwe/o-y- 'who, what' (IEW: 635)
 COGN: Lat. quis 'who', Hitt. kuis, Gr. tis 'who', Skt. kim 'what'
 SEE: *kwid 'what'
 ETYM: MBret. piou, Co. pyw are results of metathesis (*wy > yw). PCelt.
*kwe_ preserves the full grade, PIE *kWey_.This shape of the root of the
interrogative pronoun is probably older than *kwo_ (Skt. Ms, OCS kb-to
'who'), which is parallel to the demonstrative pronoun stem *so-, *to-.
Celtic unexpectedly has the full grade of the root *kwi_ (but cf. also Gr.

a secondary development within Celtic, but cf. Lat. qUI < *kWoy.
REF: LEIA C-9lf., GPC III: 2946, EIEC 456, Falileyev 134, GPC 2946f.

### *kWinut- 'crime, mistake, guilt' [Noun]
 GOID: OIr. Gin [m t]

 COGN: Skt. ctiyate 'punishes', Av. kaena 'punishment', Gr. tio 'honor',
