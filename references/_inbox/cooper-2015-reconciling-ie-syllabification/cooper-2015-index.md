# Index of Words
Forms from Indo-European languages are listed first, followed by forms from non-Indo-European
languages.
Indo-European Languages
The order of languages is as follows: Proto-Indo-European, Sanskrit, Greek (including
Mycenaean), Hittite, Latin, Iranian (Avestan, Old Persian, Ossetic), Germanic (Gothic, English,
German, Icelandic), Slavic (Church Slavonic, Polish, Russian, Serbo-Croatian), Baltic (Lithuanian,
Old Prussian), Celtic (Gaulish, Old Irish, Munster Irish, Welsh), Tocharian, Armenian.
Proto-Indo-European

plain velar counterparts, and i and u are ordered before i̯ and u̯, respectively. The use of numbers,
question marks, and parentheses follows LIV.
