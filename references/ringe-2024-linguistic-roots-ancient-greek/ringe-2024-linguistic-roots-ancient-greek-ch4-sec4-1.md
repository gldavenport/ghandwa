## 4.1 Introduction

Greek underwent no major restructuring of its inflectional morphology in its development from PIE. As in all daughters, the system of nominal inflection was reoriented around stem types defined by their final segment; as in most, it was simplified somewhat. The verb system underwent modest elaboration with little loss of inherited material. It is even harder than in the case of phonological changes to determine which changes in inflection had occurred by the time the attested dialects began to diverge; there is little evidence of relative chronology, and thus no way to distinguish early changes from later parallel developments or developments that spread from one dialect to another. In practice we are often constrained to ascribe to Proto-Greek those innovations that appear in all the dialects (including Mycenaean, so far as it offers evidence).
