# Notes — Eska 2024

## Bibliographic details

**Joseph F. Eska. "Celtic in Greek characters and implications for Greek and Celtic phonology."**  
Year: 2024. Journal/publisher: TBD — verify from PDF metadata or publication page.

## Content summary

Examines the use of theta ⟨θ⟩ and chi ⟨χ⟩ in Transalpine Celtic inscriptions engraved in Greek characters. Argues that the aspirated voiceless plosives of Greek had already become fricatives in southern Transalpine Gaul before these inscriptions were made, so theta and chi were used with their then-current Greek phonemic values (fricatives), not repurposed. Provides a terminus ante quem for Greek aspiration frication in the region, with implications for Transalpine Celtic contrasting its plosive series via [spread glottis] rather than [voice] — extending Eska 2018's laryngeal realism argument to epigraphic evidence.
