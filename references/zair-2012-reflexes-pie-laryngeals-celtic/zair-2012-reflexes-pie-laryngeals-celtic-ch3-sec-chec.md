## #Chec-

### §72. Introduction

A laryngeal in the sequence *CHEC- is lost without any reflex other than colouring an adjacent *-e-; only a representative example is given. Hoenigswald (1952) argued for the loss of laryngeals after initial *s- in Indo-European, before (phonemic) colouring of a following *-e-, on the basis of alternations like Lat. anus ‘old woman’, Hitt. h̬anna- ‘grandmother’ ~ Skt. sánaḥ ‘old’, OIr. sen ‘old’, Lat. senex ‘old man’. But *sH- is attested in forms like Hitt. išh̬iya- ‘bind’, and none of the etymologies are convincing (Polomé 1965: 32; Beekes 1969: 82–83). The idea will not be discussed further here. For the sequence *CHIC-, which has a different development from that of *CHEC-, see p. 111ff.
