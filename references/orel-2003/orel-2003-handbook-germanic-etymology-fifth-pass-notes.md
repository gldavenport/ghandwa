# Fifth-Pass Verification Notes

This pass addressed the residual issues identified after the fourth pass: very short Greek spans and ambiguous legacy-font signs.

## What changed

- Corrected page-verified KadmosNieuw Greek remnants including `¶`, `±`, `É`, `∑`, grave-vowel signs, digamma contexts, and the private-use upsilon glyph.
- Corrected the earlier `Gk ±m¤`/`Gk ±μί` value from `εἰμί` to the page-verified `ἠμί`.
- Repaired remaining Greek proper-name and short-form contexts such as `Ἀβρο-ζελμις`, `Ἀλούθαγος`, `Ἀγγείλοι`, `Ὀλόρος`, `Ῥάσκος`, `ἠέριος`, `ἠώς`, `ἦτορ`, `ἦρα`, `ἠχή`, and `ὗς`.
- Repaired Slavic hard/soft signs and nasal-vowel contexts in dictionary and index files: `˙` → `ъ`, `¸` → `ь`, and `\` → `ę`.
- Applied context-clear Sanskrit, Tocharian, Baltic, and Slavic legacy-font corrections such as `ś`, `š`, `ṣ`, `ṭ`, `ṛ`, `ṃ`, `ḍ`, `ž`, `ṅ`, `ṇ`, `ė`, and `ĩ`.
- Fixed remaining bibliographic artifacts such as `Materiały`, `Sławistyczny`, `Vṛddhi`, and related Polish/Slavic forms.
- Rebuilt the lexical-entry TSV from the corrected dictionary Markdown.

## Methodological note

This was a conservative correction pass, not a new full extraction. It used the fourth-pass files as the base and avoided broad replacement rules that would corrupt ordinary English, German, or Latin-script source text.

## Remaining caveat

The files are substantially cleaner for corpus work, but not a diplomatic critical edition. A future source-critical edition would still benefit from form-by-form page-image verification of all non-Latin scholarly transliteration.
