# Preface

This work is the revised version of my Ph. D. thesis submitted to the Fakultät für Sprach- und Literaturwissenschaften of the Ludwig-Maximilians-
Universität München in October 2004 under the title “A Morphological and Etymological Study of the Germanic Strong Verbs”. This study could not have been undertaken without all the help, support, ideas and advice I received from colleagues, friends and family, for which I am truly grateful. First and foremost, I would like to thank Prof. Theo
Vennemann, Ph.D. for being my teacher, for sparking my enthusiasm for linguistics, and for all his guidance, advice, support and criticism, which has been an invaluable contribution to my work. Furthermore, I would like to express my gratitude to my mentors and colleagues who have spent many hours discussing ideas, providing help as well as supporting me in many ways, and who are too many to name them all individually. However, in particular, I wish to thank Prof. Dr. Thomas Becker and Prof. Dr. Hans Sauer for their continuing support, PD Dr. Katrin Lindner and Dr. Patrizia Noel for their encouragement and advice, PD Dr. Eugen Hill for all his valuable help in the field of comparative linguistics, as well as Dr. Eric
Anchimbe, Debra Daniol, Stephen Laker, M.A. and Dr. Iva Welscher for proof-reading the manuscript. Moreover, thanks go Dr. Melanie Moll and the Ph.D. programme LIPP, represented by its speaker Prof. Dr. Dr. h.c. Konrad Ehlich, for supplying excellent career support and study atmosphere. Furthermore, I wish to thank an anonymous reviewer and Prof. Dr. Werner Winter for their comments on the manuscript, which have helped considerably to improve the quality of this book.
Furthermore, I would like to thank my wife, family and friends for their patience, understanding and support, which were indispensable to the completion of this book. Thanks also go to Prof. Dr. Walter Bisang and Prof. Dr. Werner Winter for opening their Series Trends in Linguistics – Studies and Monographs as well as Birgit Sievert, M.A. and Wolfgang Konwitschny at Mouton de Gruyter for their invaluable help with the publication process. Last but not least, I wish to express my heartfelt gratitude towards the Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität München for a grant with the Graduiertenförderung and towards the German Merit Foundation (Studienstiftung des Deutschen Volkes) for granting me a Ph.D. scholarship.
