RL = recent literature provides additional data

S: B, but no cognates/examples/suggestions = Seebold’s categorization is obviously
errorenous, since there is no etymological data to justify his verdict. Therefore, the
relevant verb is automatically classed as Category D, as there exists no etymology;
in most cases recent literature confirms the lack of an etymology.
