# 5 Phonology

## 5.0 Introduction

The most striking facts about Balkan languages that attracted the early attention of linguists centered on aspects of grammar, where grammar subsumed what would today be called morphology and syntax; note Kopitar’s observation cited in §2.2.1 concerning “grammar” (“Sprachform”). Nonetheless, as indicated in §4.1, much of the early attention focused on the lexicon. Yet it also became apparent that there was more to the convergence area than just grammar and lexicon, and in particular that the phonological component of these languages was also relevant.1 In this chapter we examine the evidence for phonological convergences, where “phonological” is understood in a general nontechnical sense of ‘having to do with sound.’
