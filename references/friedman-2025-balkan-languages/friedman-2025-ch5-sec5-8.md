## 5.8 Conclusion

By way of conclusion, it should suffice to note that the length of this present chapter exceeds the total number of pages on phonology in the Balkans to be found in the handbooks cited (see §5.1 and footnote 6 above, and also Table 4.1 in §4.1), and that is with much repetition of the same features across the different handbooks. Moreover, this chapter rivals in length at least, the excellent monograph by Sawicka 1997. What this means, therefore, is that there certainly is much to take note of regarding phonology in the Balkans, but the key operative notion is that one has to deal with local Balkan phonologies, not some sort of pan-Balkan phonology.
