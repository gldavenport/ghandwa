### raidit
**LATV** raidit [ai, ai, ai 2, ai 2] 'send hastily, urge, (refl.) rush', lsg. pres. raidu, lsg. pret.
           raidfju
**BSL** *roi(-
**PSL** *r[unclear]jati v. (a)
**SL** OCS rčjati (Supr.) 'push, press'; Ru. rejat' 'soar, hover, flutter'; Ukr. rijaty
           'swarm'; Bulg. reja 'wander, fly'
**PIE** *h₃roiH-
**IE** Skt. riyate 'flows, whirls'; ri1;1dti 'makes flow'; Gk. 6pivw 'excite, stir'
The Latvian verb apparently has the "Hiatustilger" -d- < *-dhhr.

<!-- source-pdf-page: 561; source-page: 547 -->
### raids
**LATV** raids 'ready, prepared'
**PIE** *h₂ roidh-
**IE** Go. garaid Nsg. n. 'arranged, comanded'; Olc. greior 'clear, ready'; MHG
           reite, gereite 'ready'
The reconstruction of the root is based on the presumed connection with forms such
as Gk. ap10μ6[unclear] 'number', which is a derivative in -0μo- from *h₂ rei- 'count' (cf. Beekes
2010: 131). Another possibility would be that we are dealing with a borrowing from
Germanic. Kroonen ( 2013: 402) does not find this appealing in view of the ablaut in
Latv. ridi 'tools' ( ....Latv. ridas) and Latv. rist 'arrange'.

**LATV** raCtklis 'scraper'
**PSL** *rydla n. o (a) 'spade, snout'

### rens
**LATV** ręns 'undicht'; VAR ręds (BW)
**BSL** *rerd(-n)-
**PSL** *rčd7Jh adj. o 'sparse, rare'
**SL** OCS rčd'lJkyję (Supr.) Apl. m. 'rare'; Ru. redkij; redok, f. redka, n. redko; Cz.
           fidkj; Slk. riedky; Pl. rzadki; USrb. rčdki; SCr. rl'jedki 'thin, sparse, rare';

Examples are ręni rudzi 'undicht stehender Roggen', ręns audums 'loses Gewebe'.

### ridas
**LATV** ridas Npl. 'allerlei Gerat, Kram'; VAR ridi Npl.
These nouns may belong to →Latv. raids, but Endzelins (ME l: 522) seems to
consider Livonian origin.

<!-- source-pdf-page: 562; source-page: 548 -->
### rist
**LATV** rist 'arrange', 1sg. pres. riedu, 1sg. pret. ridu
**IE** Go. (ga)raidjan 'arrange, command'; Olc. greioa 'settle, help'
According to Endzelins (ME l: 532), it is possible that this verb belongs to Latv. rifida
'row, line' (→rinda). In that case we would have to assume that the present stem
continues *rend- and that we are dealing with Ablautentgleisung (cf. ME: l.e.). It
seems simpler to seek a connection with →Latv. raids.
