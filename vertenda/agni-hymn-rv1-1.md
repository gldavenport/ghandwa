# RV 1.1 — First Agni Hymn (*Agnim īḷe*)

**Source:** Rigveda 1.1, attributed to Madhucchandas Vaiśvāmitra. The opening hymn of the entire Rigveda. Public domain.

**Why this text:** The first hymn of the Rigveda is addressed to Agni — directly cognate with Ghandwa's Agnis. The vocabulary is dense with PIE roots traceable into Ghandwa. Renders in the highest register of the Sacred function. A Ghandwa translation is not merely a benchmark but an act of mythological continuity: the same deity, the same praise formula, in a sister tradition.

---

## Source Text (Devanagari)

> अग्निमीळे पुरोहितं यज्ञस्य देवमृत्विजम् ।
> होतारं रत्नधातमम् ॥१॥
>
> अग्निः पूर्वेभिरृषिभिरीड्यो नूतनैरुत ।
> स देवाँ एह वक्षति ॥२॥
>
> अग्निना रयिमश्नवत् पोषमेव दिवे-दिवे ।
> यशसं वीरवत्तमम् ॥३॥
>
> अग्ने यं यज्ञमध्वरं विश्वतः परिभूरसि ।
> स इद्देवेषु गच्छति ॥४॥
>
> अग्निर्होता कविक्रतुः सत्यश्चित्रश्रवस्तमः ।
> देवो देवेभिरागमत् ॥५॥
>
> यदङ्ग दाशुषे त्वमग्ने भद्रं करिष्यसि ।
> तवेत्तत् सत्यमङ्गिरः ॥६॥
>
> उप त्वाग्ने दिवे-दिवे दोषावस्तर्धिया वयम् ।
> नमो भरन्त एमसि ॥७॥
>
> राजन्तमध्वराणां गोपामृतस्य दीदिविम् ।
> वर्धमानं स्वे दमे ॥८॥
>
> स नः पितेव सूनवे ऽग्ने सूपायनो भव ।
> सचस्वा नः स्वस्तये ॥९॥

## English Translation (after Geldner / Jamison & Brereton)

> 1. I praise Agni, the household priest, the divine officiant of the sacrifice, the invoker, best bestower of wealth.
> 2. Agni, worthy of being praised by the ancient seers and by the new — may he bring the gods here.
> 3. Through Agni may one obtain wealth and prosperity day by day, glorious and most abounding in heroic sons.
> 4. Agni, the sacrifice and ritual work that you surround on every side — that one goes to the gods.
> 5. Agni the invoker, wise of purpose, true, most renowned — a god, he comes with the gods.
> 6. Whatever good you will do for the one who presses Soma, Agni — that of you is true, Aṅgiras.
> 7. To you, Agni, we come day after day — we who bear homage — with our thought, at nightfall and at dawn.
> 8. (To you) who reign over sacrifices, guardian of ṛta, shining bright, growing in your own house.
> 9. Be easy of access to us, like a father to his son. Stay with us, Agni, for our well-being.
