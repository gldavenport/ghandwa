# The King and the God

**Source:** Sen, Subhadra Kumar, ed. 1994. "Proto-Indo-European: A Multiangular View." *Journal of Indo-European Studies* 22(1–2): 67–89/90. Multi-contributor piece; specific reconstruction used should be identified by contributor when the article is in hand.

**Why this text:** The shortest reconstructed PIE narrative with solid cross-branch attestation. Tests question-and-answer dialogue and the ritual exchange formula (*quid pro quo*). The exchange formula (*kʷid moi deh₃s* / *kʷid moi deh₃mi*) is directly relevant to Ghandwa's Sacred register.

**Note on source text:** The PIE reconstruction below is a working paraphrase. Verify exact wording against the JIES article before drafting translation.

---

## Source Text (PIE reconstruction, approximate)

> *h₃rḗǵs h₁nḗr diwós pótis h₁esti.*
> *só kʷid wekʷt: "diw, su moi putlóm deh₃s."*
> *só diws wekʷt: "kʷid moi deh₃s?"*
> *só wekʷt: "gʷh₃én moi ékʷom deh₃mi."*

---

## English Translation

> A king was a man, a lord of the god.
> He said: "God, give me a son."
> The god said: "What will you give me?"
> He said: "I will give you my finest horse."
