# The King and the God

**Source:** Calvert Watkins, *How to Kill a Dragon: Aspects of Indo-European Poetics* (Oxford University Press, 1995), Chapter 1. Reconstructed from parallel Vedic (*Aitareya Brāhmaṇa*) and Italic (*Iguvine Tables*) cognate texts. Project reference copy at `references/watkins-1995/watkins-1995-clean-pass-4.md`.

**Why this text:** The shortest reconstructed PIE narrative with solid cross-branch attestation. Tests question-and-answer dialogue and the ritual exchange formula (*quid pro quo*). The exchange formula Watkins reconstructs here (*kʷid moi deh₃s* / *kʷid moi deh₃mi*) is the same structural core he traces through the dragon-slaying corpus — directly relevant to Ghandwa's mythological register.

**Note on source text:** The PIE reconstruction below is a working paraphrase of the narrative structure. Verify exact wording against `references/watkins-1995/watkins-1995-clean-pass-4.md` before drafting translation; the chapter text may differ in detail.

## Source Text (Watkins' PIE reconstruction, approximate)

> *h₃rḗǵs h₁nḗr diwós pótis h₁esti.*
> *só kʷid wekʷt: "diw, su moi putlóm deh₃s."*
> *só diws wekʷt: "kʷid moi deh₃s?"*
> *só wekʷt: "gʷh₃én moi ékʷom deh₃mi."*

---

## English Translation

> A king was a man, a lord of the god.
> He said: "God, give me a son."
> The god said: "What will you give me?"
> He said: "I will give you my finest horse."
