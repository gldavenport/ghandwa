# The One Ring Verse

**Source:** J.R.R. Tolkien, *The Lord of the Rings* (1954). © Estate of J.R.R. Tolkien. Included here for private linguistic use only; do not redistribute.

**Why this text:** Metrically constrained; forces demonstration of Ghandwa prosody. The verse is a binding formula — Sacred/Martial register. Tolkien was a historical linguist; the choice is defensible in a comparative IE context.

---

## Source Text

> Three Rings for the Elven-kings under the sky,
> Seven for the Dwarf-lords in their halls of stone,
> Nine for Mortal Men doomed to die,
> One for the Dark Lord on his dark throne
> In the Land of Mordor where the Shadows lie.
> One Ring to rule them all, One Ring to find them,
> One Ring to bring them all, and in the darkness bind them,
> In the Land of Mordor where the Shadows lie.
