# Second Merseburg Charm (*Phol ende Uuodan*)

**Source:** Old High German, preserved in a 10th-century manuscript in the Merseburg Cathedral Chapter Library. Likely earlier in origin. Public domain.

**Why this text:** One of two surviving pre-Christian OHG magical texts. Directly attests IE religious material: the healing formula, the bone-to-bone charm structure, and the divine names (Wodan/Odin, Balder/Phol, Friia/Freya, Sinthgunt) are all IE-relevant. Tests the enchantment/healing register — a distinct sub-register of Sacred that Ghandwa will need for the Agnis mythology (specifically the Smith and Ash-Gatherer aspects in healing roles). The charm formula (*bēn zi bēna, bluot zi bluoda*) is a direct structural parallel to the kind of liturgical formulas that would appear in Ghandwa corpus texts.

---

## Source Text (OHG)

> Phol ende Uuodan
> fuorun zi holza.
> du uuart demo Balderes uolon
> sin uuoz birenkit.
> thu biguol en Sinthgunt,
> Sunna era suister;
> thu biguol en Friia,
> Uolla era suister;
> thu biguol en Uuodan,
> so he uuola conda:
> sose benrenki,
> sose bluotrenki,
> sose lidirenki:
> bēn zi bēna,
> bluot zi bluoda,
> lid zi geliden,
> sose gelimida sin.

---

## English Translation

> Phol and Wodan were riding to the forest.
> Then Balder's foal sprained its foot.
> Then Sinthgunt charmed it, Sunna her sister;
> then Friia charmed it, Volla her sister;
> then Wodan charmed it, as he well could:
> as the bone-sprain,
> as the blood-sprain,
> as the limb-sprain:
> bone to bone,
> blood to blood,
> limb to limb,
> so be they fitted together.
