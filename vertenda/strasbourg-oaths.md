# The Strasbourg Oaths

**Source:** Nithard, *Histoire des fils de Louis le Pieux*, Book III. 842 AD. Earliest dated text in Old French; also a major early Old High German document. Public domain.

**Why this text:** The canonical "first document" of a Romance vernacular. Formulaic, politically charged, short. Fits the Sacred/Legal register naturally. A Ghandwa rendering functions as the language's own "first document" — the conlang-equivalent of the Oaths' historical role.

**Which version to translate:** The Old French version (Louis's oath, *Pro Deo amur*) is the primary target. The OHG version is included for reference and comparative interest.

---

## Source Text

### Old French (Louis's oath — primary translation target)

> Pro Deo amur et pro christian poblo et nostro commun salvament, dist di in avant, in quant Deus savir et podir me dunat, si salvarai eo cist meon fradre Karlo et in aiudha et in cadhuna cosa, si cum om per dreit son fradra salvar dift, in o quid il mi altresi fazet, et ab Ludher nul plaid numquam prindrai qui meon vol cist meon fradre Karle in damno sit.

### Old High German (Charles's oath — secondary / comparative)

> In godes minna ind in thes christianes folches ind unser bedhero gehaltnissi, fon thesemo dage frammordes, so fram so mir Got gewizci indi mahd furgibit, so haldih thesan minan bruodher, soso man mit rehtu sinan bruodher scal, in thiu thaz er mig so sama duo, indi mit Ludheren in nohheiniu thing ne gegango the minan willon imo ce scadhen werhen.

---

## English Translation

> For the love of God and for the Christian people and our common salvation, from this day forward, insofar as God grants me knowledge and power, I will aid this my brother Charles both in his aid and in every thing, as one ought by right to aid one's brother, provided that he does likewise for me; and with Lothair I will never enter into any agreement which, by my will, may be to the harm of this my brother Charles.
