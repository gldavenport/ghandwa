## Dendronym suffixes (*-dr-*, *-n-*)

Status: `pending` — proposals only; no lexicon entries yet

Source: Working notes.

### Proposal 1 — *-dr-* as a zero-grade dendronym suffix

Hypothesis: *-dr-* is a zero-grade lexical suffix derived from the *dóru ~ déru* complex (PIE \**dér-u* 'tree, wood'), functioning as a tree-name formant. Parallel to the productive use of the root in compounds (cf. *gvetudoru* 'pine tree', lexicon #101).

Candidate forms:

| Ghandwa output | gloss | notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| \* *perkudervis* | 'oak-tree' | u-stem base *pérkus* + *-dr-* + ā-stem ending; feminine as tree name |
| \**salikdervis* | 'willow-tree' | base *salik-* (etymology unclear)|

### Proposal 2 — *-n-* as a parallel dendronym suffix

Comparandum from Kroonen (EDPG): PGmc \**ahurnaz* < \**akr̥-no-*, cognate with Latin (form truncated in notes). Suggests *-no-* as a productive tree/plant name suffix in the branch.

Candidate forms (PIE preforms; Ghandwa outputs not yet worked out):

| PIE preform | gloss | notes |
|---|---|---|
| \**salik-n-os* | 'willow' | *-n-* variant of the *salik-* base; cf. *salikdros* above |
| \**akr̥-n-os* | 'acorn(-tree)? / oak' | Kroonen's \**ahurnaz*; Ghandwa output ~*akarnos* by syllabic resonant rule |
| \**aik-* | ? | base attested in notes; form and gloss incomplete |
| \**kosVl-* | 'hazel?' | base attested in notes; vowel of second syllable unresolved; cf. Kroonen \**hasla-* < \**kos-(e)l-o* (EDPG) |

Open questions:
- *-neh₂-* (fem.) and *-neh₂-* vs. *-dreh₂-* comparison flagged in source notes but not yet developed.
- The alder word (*alsno-* etc.) noted in Round 5 (Weiss) as showing remarkable morphological variation — possibly relevant to the *-n-* suffix question.

## *amg-* fossil compounding stem from \*meǵh₂- — pending

*Added 2026-03-15.*

The u-stem adjective *megús* ~ *megóus* "big, great" (#424) derives from PIE \*meǵh₂-u-s with strong-stem leveling (full-grade \*meǵ- generalized throughout). The zero-grade root \*m̥ǵh₂- would have surfaced as *amg-* in Ghandwa (syllabic m̥ → am, centum ǵ → g, h₂ drops prevocalically or vocalizes to a before consonant).

Before the paradigm leveled, the zero-grade oblique stem was available as a compounding element. Some pre-Ghandwa formations using *amg-* may survive as frozen compounds in which the connection to *megús* is opaque to speakers.

**Target domains:** royal and religious epithets.

**Candidate compound types:**
- *amg-* + deity/power noun → divine epithet ("the great [X]")
- *amg-* + rule/king noun → royal title ("great-king" or similar)
- Possible parallel to Latin *ingēns* (< \*n̥- + \*ǵn̥h₁-ent-s): a zero-grade root frozen inside a compound that synchronically looks monomorphemic

**Status:** 🔲 parked — compound(s) to be coined when specific inscription, lore, or naming needs arise. No rush; the live adjective *megús* is in the lexicon.
