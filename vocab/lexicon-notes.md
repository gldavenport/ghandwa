## mēks

**PIE source:** \*méǵh₂-s ~ \*mǵh₂-és 'great, mighty' (root adjective/noun; the same root as Gk. μέγας, Lat. *magnus*)

**Phonological derivation (regular sound changes):**

1. Nom.sg. \*méǵh₂-s: h₂ is lost between consonants → \*mégs. Cluster \*gs → ks by voicing assimilation (cf. Lat. *rēx* < \*rēg-s, *lēx* < \*lēg-s) → **meks**.
2. Obl. \*mǵh₂-és: syllabic \*m̥ → *am* (Ghandwa CaRC rule); h₂ between the following consonant and vowel colors the vowel \*e → \*a and is then lost → **\*amgás** (or dialectally \*amagás). This form is phonologically opaque and morphologically non-paradigmatic.

**Analogical repair (two steps):**

1. **Oblique leveling.** The inherited oblique \*amgás is replaced by \*megés, formed by attraction to the productive -ks ~ -gés stem class (cf. *rēks ~ regés*). The generalized stem is *meg-*, extracted from nom. *meks*. This is straightforward paradigm-class attraction; the -ks ~ -gés class provides both the model and the justification.

2. **Direct-case lengthening.** Nom. *meks* → **mēks** by the pattern of long direct-case vowels in the -ks ~ -gés class (cf. *rēks* with long ē against short obl. *regés*). Quantity is borrowed from the class pattern; vowel quality ē is preserved from the root \*e-grade. Speakers perceive the synchronic alternation (monosyllabic nom. with long vowel ~ oblique with short) as a class property, not as an etymological fact.

**Register note:** mēks is archaic/formal register. It functions as a frozen epithet or title (*X Mēks* = "X the Great / X the Mighty") rather than as a productive adjective meaning 'big'. The productive adjective for 'big, large' comes from a separate Caland or suffixed derivative of the same root (not yet entered).

**Root anchor:** *meg-* is the canonical Ghandwa stem for this root. All further derivations build from *meg-*, not from the suppletive oblique base.

## megō

**Derivation:** Appellative -ōn-stem formed from the generalized stem *meg-* (established by the mēks paradigm repair above). PIE \*meǵ-ōn- → Ghandwa *megō* (nom.sg., with final \*-n dropped as in other -ōn-stems; cf. *rēks* class behavior). Gen.sg. \*meǵ-on-és → *megonés*.

**PIE type:** The -ōn-/-on- stem is a well-attested IE appellative/title formation derived from adjective roots (cf. Lat. *bibō* 'drinker', Germanic -an- name stems, Gk. personal name formations in -ων). The paradigm is treated as a leveled -ōn- stem (o-grade throughout the suffix) rather than the amphikinetic alternating type.

**Semantic:** Primarily appellative — a standing social or title designation: "one characterized by greatness/might." The participative reading ("one who is great") is latent in the morphology. Not singulative (the base is not a mass noun or collective).

**Relation to mēks:** mēks is the predicate/epithet (*X Mēks* = "X the Great"); *megō* is the count/appellative noun (*megō* = "a great one, a lord"). The pair covers the same semantic space that Latin splits between *magnus* (adj.) and later *magnates* (substantivized pl.).
